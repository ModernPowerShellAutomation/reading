Esta metáfora del gran libro del universo aún permanece entre nosotros.

This metaphor of the great book of the universe still remains among us.

Pensamos en el universo como un «libro» cuyos secretos intentamos desvelar y seguimos utilizando las matemáticas para hacerlo.

We think of the universe as a "book" whose secrets we try to reveal and continue to use mathematics to do so.

La importancia de Arquímedes para la historia de la ciencia consiste en haber demostrado la manera en que esta metáfora literalmente podía funcionar.

The importance of Archimedes for the history of science is to have demonstrated how this metaphor could literally work.

The first to decipher the book of the universe was him, and discovered that it was written in the language of mathematics.

En 1623, cuando Galileo escribió estas palabras, todos los manuscritos de Arquímedes ya habían desaparecido.

In 1623, when Galileo wrote these words, all Archimedes manuscripts had already disappeared.

Codex B was lost at some point in the fourteenth century and Codex A, in the course of the 16th century, probably when Galileo was still a child.

Sólo quedaba una copia, pero estaba escondida de la vista.

There was only one copy, but I was hidden from the view.

The monks who used it never learned to read their triangles, their circles and their other geometric figures.

In 1687, when the work of Isaac Newton was published, he begins, this codex - that is, the Archimedes palimpsest - was still in the Holy Land, hidden in sight.

He was a world of distance from the British scientist who was enclaustrated in his rooms of Trinity College, in Cambridge.
