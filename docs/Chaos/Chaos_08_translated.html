Joseph Ford, of the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Technological Institute), quoted Tolstoy: «I know that most of the men, including those who are at ease in the face of very unrevested problems, on rare occasions they accept the simplest and most evident truth, if it forces them to confess the falsehood of the conclusions that have been delighted to expose their colleagues, they have taught their colleagues. and that they have warmed, thread after thread, in the texture of their lives ».

The caoticists or caologists (words like these sounded) appeared frequently disproportionate in the annual lists of pensioners and important awards.

An academic dissemination process had placed, mid -1980, chaos specialists in influential positions within university administrations.

Centers and institutes were founded to specialize in "non -linear dynamics" and "complex systems."

It has become an experimental science for researchers and mathematicians, in which the computer replaces laboratories full of test tubes and microscopes.

"For the mathematician it is a masochism to work without images," said an expert in chaos.

How will intuition develop?

Some carry out their work explicitly denying that it is a revolution; Others take advantage of the Kuhn vocabulary of paradigmatic removals, in order to describe the alterations they witness.

The first articles about chaos remembered, in terms of style, the time of Benjamin Franklin, since they returned to the original principles.

Kuhn comments that the established sciences assume a mass of knowledge, which serves in research as a starting point for all.

In order not to bore their colleagues, scientists begin and routinely end their publications with esoteric things.

New hopes, new styles and, more importantly, new way of seeing.

Revolutions are not gradually declared.

An interpretation of nature replaces another.

Something that is as if an entire industry changed all its supplies to manufacture novel products.

As Kuhn describes it: "It is something like if the professional community had been transported suddenly to another planet, in which family objects are contemplated differently and they add, at the same time, other strangers."

Relojes de péndulo, «bolas espaciales» y columpios La rata de laboratorio de la nueva ciencia fue el péndulo: emblema de la mecánica clásica, ejemplo de actividad contenida y epítome de regularidad cronométrica.

Pendulum watches, "space balls" and swings The laboratory rat of the new science was the pendulum: emblem of classical mechanics, example of contained activity and epitome of chronometric regularity.

Una pesa en forma de lenteja se halla al cabo de una péndola.

A lentil -shaped weight is at the end of a pendola.

¿Hay algo más distante de la extravagancia de la turbulencia?

Is there anything more distant from the extravagance of turbulence?

Como Arquímedes su baño y Newton su manzana, Galileo tuvo, según la leyenda tan decantada como sospechosa, una lámpara de iglesia, que oscilaba de acá para allá, una y otra vez, sin tregua, enviando un monótono mensaje a su consciencia.

Christian Huygens made the predictability of the pendulum the means of marking time and put Western civilization on a road without return.

Foucault, en el Panthéon de París, utilizó uno de veinte pisos de alto para demostrar la rotación terrestre.

Foucault, in the Panthéon in Paris, used one of twenty high floors to demonstrate the terrestrial rotation.

All wall and wrist watches (to the era of the vibrator quartz) were tried with variable size and configuration pendulums.

En el espacio, exento de fricción, el movimiento periódico se tiene en las órbitas de los cuerpos celestes; pero en la Tierra cualquier oscilación regular se debe a algún pariente del péndulo.

In space, exempt from friction, the periodic movement is in the orbits of the celestial bodies; But on Earth any regular oscillation is due to some relative of the pendulum.

Los circuitos electrónicos básicos se describen con ecuaciones que son, puntualmente, las mismas que importan a la lenteja pendular.

The basic electronic circuits are described with equations that are, promptly, the same that matter to the pendular lentil.

Las oscilaciones electrónicas son millones de veces más raudas, pero idénticas a él desde el punto de vista físico.

Electronic oscillations are millions of times more rapid, but identical to it from the physical point of view.

However, towards the twentieth century, classical mechanics was reserved for classrooms and routine engineering projects.

The pendulums decorated the science museums and encouraged the gift stores of the airports in the form of "space balls" plastic.

Aristotle, when observing the pendulum, saw a weight that tried to go to the earth and that was balanced with violence from one place to another, because his rope prevented him.

The physical movement, according to Aristotle, was neither an amount nor a force, but a change, such as the growth of an individual.

A weight that falls seeks nothing more than its most natural state, the state it will reach if you abandon yourself.

Within its context, Aristotle's opinion was not crazy.

Galileo surpassed the ancient Greeks, but not in the possession of better data.

Galileo perceived regularity because he had already formulated a theory that predicted it.
