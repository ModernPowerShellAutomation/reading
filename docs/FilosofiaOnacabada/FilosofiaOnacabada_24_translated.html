The one who does the affront to God to claim his radical freedom, who claims to have no other law than himself, is the avenger of the death of the Father, is the one who has acted, even through the murder of Egisto and Climenestra, in favor of honor and to free the city from his guilt and ignominy.

The moral horizon of a philosophy of radical freedom and the absence of the foundation of existence cannot be other than free commitment, also taken from its absolute lack of foundation.

Likewise, the commitment is not the effect of an objective situation, but the free choice to deny and transcend it.

But the commitment, this pure act, the result of a free and absolute will, in which the commitment of Sartrean existentialist morality is played, can it base a common praxis?

Se hace teniendo en cuenta a los otros, pero ¿puede hacerse con los otros?

It is done taking into account others, but can it be done with others?

Estas preguntas ponen sobre la mesa dos de las cuestiones que atravesarán y tensarán el trabajo filosófico de Sartre en las décadas siguientes y, especialmente, en la siguiente gran obra después de El ser y la nada, que es La crítica de la razón dialéctica (1960).

These questions put on the table two of the issues that will cross and tighten the philosophical work of Sartre in the following decades and, especially, in the next great work after being and nothingness, which is the criticism of the dialectical reason (1960).

Entering these issues more in depth leads to how the Sartreano ontological apparatus, built in its reinterpretation of Husserlian phenomenology and the Heideggerian world-world-being in view of a view to based on a radical and irreducible conception of freedom, makes it very difficult to think about a common praxis, as well as the idea of a meeting between freedoms and between consciences.

In the end, Sartre himself seems convicted of acting as Orestes, only with his free conscience, in the middle of the infested city.

La filosofía del nosotros Pensando con Maurice Merleau-Ponty En un ensayo titulado «El filósofo y su sombra», dedicado a Husserl, Merleau-Ponty presenta a su maestro como un buceador que, zambulléndose en el pozo de su conciencia, sale a mar abierto y descubre el mundo.

The philosophy of us Thinking with Maurice Merleau-Ponty In an essay entitled "The philosopher and his shadow", dedicated to Husserl, Merleau-Ponty presents his teacher as a diver who, diving in the well of his conscience, goes out to the open sea and discovers the world.

«Redescubre la identidad del «entrar en sí» y «salir de sí» que para Hegel definía el «absoluto»1 pero en ese movimiento de entrada y de salida emerge no el punto de vista de la totalidad autotransparente sino el de un horizonte inacabado, con sombras y opacidades.

"Rediscover the identity of" entering itself "and" getting out "that for Hegel defined the" absolute "1 but in that movement of entry and exit emerges not the point of view of the self -transparent totality but that of an unfinished horizon, with shadows and opacities.

El mar abierto de Husserl es el mundo de la vida.

Husserl's open sea is the world of life.

La filosofía de la conciencia apunta así, más allá de sí misma, a una rehabilitación ontológica del ser sensible.

The philosophy of consciousness points to itself, beyond itself, to an ontological rehabilitation of sensitive being.

Pero Merleau-Ponty nos avisa: este camino es el impensado de Husserl, la sombra que proyectan las luces de lo que efectivamente pensó.

But Merleau-Ponty warns us: this path is Husserl's unthinkable, the shadow that projects the lights of what he effectively thought.

Recorrer los márgenes e indicios de esta sombra, de este impensado, es lo que Merleau-Ponty se propone.

Touring the margins and signs of this shadow, of this unthinkable, is what Merleau-Ponty proposes.

Llevar hasta sus últimas consecuencias esta filosofía que en vez de sobrevolar el mundo se zambulle en él para encontrar su secreto: «El secreto del mundo que buscamos tiene que estar contenido en mi contacto con él».

Take this philosophy to its last consequences that instead of flying over the world to zambulle in it to find its secret: "The secret of the world we are looking for has to be contained in my contact with him."

"The unjustifiable certainty of a world that is common to us is for us the basis of truth" 3.

Pensar esta certeza injustificable pone en marcha un pensamiento que hace de la fenomenología la puerta de entrada a una ontología del ser sensible, expresivo y común.

Thinking about this unjustifiable certainty lays a thought that makes phenomenology the entrance door to an ontology of being sensitive, expressive and common.

Una filosofía de la inmanencia, una filosofía de la promiscuidad.

A philosophy of immanence, a philosophy of promiscuity.

En todo caso, una filosofía que lleva la fenomenología del Yo y de la conciencia a su propia imposibilidad.

In any case, a philosophy that carries the phenomenology of the self and of consciousness to its own impossibility.

Merleau-Ponty realiza el impensado husserliano: la conquista del ser sensible, no como ámbito parcial del ser sino como ámbito de la ontología.

Merleau-Ponty performs the unthinkable Husserliano: the conquest of the sensitive being, not as a partial scope of being but as a scope of ontology.

Merleau-Ponty puede dar el vuelco a la filosofía de la conciencia y encaminarse hacia otra relación con la verdad porque pone en la raíz del pensamiento filosófico la experiencia del nosotros.

Merleau-Ponty can turn the philosophy of consciousness and head towards another relationship with the truth because it puts the experience of us at the root of philosophical thought.

Su zambullida, a diferencia de la de Husserl, no se da desde la conciencia de un Ego singular, sino en el campo de relaciones de un Yo puesto en plural.

His dive, unlike Husserl's, is not given from the awareness of a unique ego, but in the field of relations of a plural.

Our relationship with the true goes through others.

Or we go to the real ones with them, or it is not towards the true where we are going.

There is no truth without them, but it is not enough to be with them to achieve the truth.

4 El error del alter ego La intersubjetividad es un problema típicamente moderno, propio de una filosofía que ya ha hecho del sujeto y de su conciencia el pilar central y soberano de la metafísica.

4 The alter ego error Intersubjectivity is a typically modern problem, typical of a philosophy that has already made the subject and its conscience the central and sovereign pillar of metaphysics.

Y las filosofías de la existencia lo reciben a través de dos planteamientos básicos: en el siglo XIX, a través del modelo hegeliano de la lucha entre conciencias, y en el siglo XX, a través de las paradojas husserlianas sobre el acoplamiento trascendental y la comunidad fenomenológica.

And the philosophies of existence are received through two basic approaches: in the nineteenth century, through the Hegelian model of the struggle between consciences, and in the twentieth century, through the Husserlian paradoxes on the transcendental coupling and the phenomenological community.

Que la subjetividad es intersubjetiva es pensado, en el primer caso, desde un modelo dialéctico en el que la negatividad de la relación entre las conciencias apunta, a través de la lucha, la dominación y el reconocimiento, a un nosotros final.

That subjectivity is intersubjective is thought, in the first case, from a dialectical model in which the negativity of the relationship between consciences points, through struggle, domination and recognition, to a final us.

En el segundo caso, a través de un modelo fenomenológico para cuyas descripciones de la corriente de vivencias puras de la conciencia el nosotros sólo puede emerger como una paradoja, en la tensión entre el análisis trascendental y la fidelidad a la experiencia.

In the second case, through a phenomenological model for whose descriptions of the current of pure experiences we can only emerge as a paradox, in the tension between the transcendental analysis and the faithfulness to the experience.

Merleau-Ponty tiene la audacia de romper de raíz el problema de la intersubjetividad como problema de acceso (sea conflictivo o armónico) al otro.

Merleau-Ponty has the audacity to break the problem of intersubjectivity as a problem of access (whether conflicting or harmonious) to the other.

No se propone encontrar una buena y definitiva solución al problema del solipsismo, sino mostrar que el problema mismo es fruto de un error, que es un falso problema.

It is not proposed to find a good and definitive solution to the problem of solipsism, but to show that the problem itself is the result of an error, which is a false problem.

El punto de partida de este error es el que comete la filosofía de la conciencia o del sujeto cuando pone al otro «ante mí», como esa conciencia que se esconde tras el objeto que enfrento y que percibo como el cuerpo o como el rostro de otro.

The starting point of this error is the one that makes the philosophy of consciousness or the subject when it puts the other "before me", like that consciousness that is hidden behind the object I face and that I perceive as the body or as the face of another.

«El otro ante mí» es la trampa ante la cual, una vez tendida, la filosofía no puede sino sucumbir.

"The other before me" is the trap before which, once laid, philosophy can only succumb.

Nunca se podrá comprender que el otro aparezca ante nosotros; lo que hay ante nosotros es un objeto.

You can never understand that the other appears before us; What is before us is an object.

La filosofía reflexiva nos condena a la alteridad radical.

Reflective philosophy condemns us to radical otherness.

«No sólo reprochamos a la filosofía reflexiva haber convertido el mundo en noema, sino también haber desfigurado el sujeto de la reflexión al concebirlo como pensante y haber hecho impensables sus relaciones con otros sujetos en un mundo que les es común.

«Not only do we reproach reflective philosophy to have turned the world into noma, but also to have disfigured the subject of reflection by conceiving it as a thought and having made its relations with other subjects unthinkable in a world that is common to them.

»5 Para el imperialismo del «yo pienso», la pluralidad de conciencias siempre será un escándalo.

»5 For the imperialism of "I think," the plurality of consciences will always be a scandal.

Incluso cuando, como en el caso de Sartre, la conciencia se ha vaciado de toda positividad, el yo ha sido defenestrado y sólo queda su mirada.

Even when, as in the case of Sartre, consciousness has emptied all positivity, the self has been defenestrated and only his gaze remains.

A pesar de su larga y difícil amistad, de su andadura compartida en Temps modernes y de su continuo diálogo filosófico, Merleau-Ponty no deja nunca de denunciar el precio filosófico y político del dualismo sartreano del en-sí y del para-sí.

Despite his long and difficult friendship, his shared journey in Temps Modernes and his continuous philosophical dialogue, Merleau-Ponty never stops denouncing the philosophical and political price of Sartrean dualism of the EN-Yes and the para-yes.

Sartre no puede dar razón, filosóficamente, de la intersubjetividad: en su filosofía sólo puede haber una pluralidad de sujetos en lucha, pero nunca una verdadera experiencia del otro.

Sartre cannot give reason, philosophically, of intersubjectivity: in his philosophy there can only be a plurality of subjects in struggle, but never a true experience of the other.

Sartre mismo deja clara su postura: «el conflicto es el sentido original del ser-para-otro»6 y el nosotros sólo puede ser una experiencia psicológica, subjetiva y circunstancial de la estructura ontológica del ser-para-otro.

Sartre itself makes its position clear: «The conflict is the original sense of being-for-another» 6 and we can only be a psychological, subjective and circumstantial experience of the ontological structure of the being-for-another.

De esta disputa filosófica de raíz ontológica se derivarán todas las diferencias políticas concretas y coyunturales que conducen finalmente a la ruptura entre Sartre y Merleau-Ponty.

From this philosophical dispute of ontological root all the concrete and short-term political differences that finally lead to the break between Sartre and Merleau-Ponty will be derived.

When the social can only enter philosophy through the alter ego, from the relationship between consciences, neither the situation nor the common action can be thought.

Every common dimension is reduced to the effect of violence, a conquest of one freedom over another and politics, to a decision of mandarins.

Merleau-Ponty Emprende, philosophically and politically, another way.

A way capable of accounting for an action sustained by categories and by a relationship with the world and with the others that the philosophy of the self and the other cannot explain.

La reformulación de la cuestión de la intersubjetividad está en el centro de esta otra vía.

The reformulation of the question of intersubjectivity is in the center of this other way.

It is not about explaining my access to the other but our involvement in a common world.

I think about the self, the primacy of the separation to the priority of the link and beyond the Yo-another scheme, subject-object, Merleau-Ponty points a new problem that will have long-range philosophical consequences.

Explicar ya no la relación entre individuos sino la imposibilidad de ser un individuo será el cometido de una filosofía que tendrá que replantear, de raíz, el sentido de la palabra filosófica y de su pregunta por el ser.

Explaining no longer the relationship between individuals but the impossibility of being an individual will be the task of a philosophy that will have to rethink, root, the meaning of the philosophical word and his question for being.

Going beyond the perspective of Cartesian cogito, only before the world and in front of others, means to Merleau-Ponty discover in a situation, learn to think from the collective perspective of an "ON", in French, of a primordial impersonal plural.
