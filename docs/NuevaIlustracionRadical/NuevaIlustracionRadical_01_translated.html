If Kant, in 1784, announced that European societies were, then, in times of illustration, we can say today that we are, on the entire planet, in times of anti -ustration.

He used the term in a dynamic sense: illustration was not a state, it was a task.

We are also: antiilustration is not a state, it is a war.

The faces of this anti -nilustrated war are many and multiply day by day.

Politically, an authoritarian desire that has made despotism and violence grows a new mobilization force.

It can be called populism, but it is a confusing term.

What is about a new authoritarianism that permeates the entire society.

From all areas, what triumphs is a fascination for the premodern: everything that had "before" was better.

As Zygmunt Bauman explained in his posthumous book, it is the refuge in what he calls "featopias", that is, in utopias that are projected in a idealized past: from tribal life to the extolling of any pre -colonial way of life, by the mere fact of being.

La educación, el saber y la ciencia se hunden también, hoy, en un desprestigio del que solo pueden salvarse si se muestran capaces de ofrecer soluciones concretas a la sociedad: soluciones laborales, soluciones técnicas, soluciones económicas.

Education, knowledge and science are also sinking, today, in a discredit that can only be saved if they are able to offer specific solutions to society: work solutions, technical solutions, economic solutions.

Solutionism is the alibi of a knowledge that has lost the attribution of becoming better, as people and as a society.

We no longer believe in it and that's why we ask for solutions and nothing more than solutions.

We no longer have ourselves better but only in obtaining more or less privileges at a time that does not go anywhere, because it has given up aiming at a better future.

The anti -nilustrated war legitimizes a social, cultural and political regime based on voluntary credulity.

The radical illustration was a fight against credulity, from confidence in human nature to emancipate and become better.

There is a distance between the civilizational project of domination and the critical commitment to the emancipation that needs to be explored again.

Después de la Segunda Guerra Mundial, Adorno y Horkheimer escribieron su famoso epitafio sobre el presente en Dialéctica de la Ilustración: La Ilustración, en el más amplio sentido de pensamiento en continuo progreso, ha perseguido desde siempre el objetivo de liberar a los hombres del miedo y constituirlos en señores.

Given the current magnitude of calamity, which has put the human species itself on the edge of its sustainability, perhaps it has come to unravel the implications of this sentence and this double identification.
