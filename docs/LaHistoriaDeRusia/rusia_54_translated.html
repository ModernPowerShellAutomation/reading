The rest of the republics, Ukraine especially, already distrusted any type of union with Moscow, the power center of the KGB and the Armed Forces.

In practice, it was a coup d'etat of the leaders of the three republics.

In a farewell speech transmitted from Kremlin on Christmas Day, Gorbachev declared that he could not support the abolition of the Soviet Union.

11 FINES ¿Cómo acaba la historia de Rusia?

11 FINNISH How does the story of Russia end?

¿Hasta qué punto estará su futuro determinado por su pasado?

To what extent will your future determined by your past?

En muchos sentidos, el país parece estar atrapado en un ciclo en que la historia se repite.

In many ways, the country seems to be caught in a cycle in which history is repeated.

El Estado autocrático se derrumbó dos veces durante el siglo XX, en 1917 y 1991, y en ambas ocasiones volvió a renacer con una forma distinta.

The autocratic state collapsed twice during the twentieth century, in 1917 and 1991, and on both occasions it was reborn again with a different form.

Las fuerzas públicas que se activaron a partir de la crisis del Estado han resultado ser demasiado débiles y estar demasiado divididas como para sostener un Gobierno democrático.

Public forces that were activated from the state crisis have turned out to be too weak and be too divided to sustain a democratic government.

Este ha sido un patrón recurrente en toda la historia de Rusia.

This has been a recurring pattern throughout the history of Russia.

El Estado autocrático se ha visto muchas veces retado por revueltas populares, pero siempre ha conseguido restablecer su poder.

The autocratic state has been challenged by popular revolts, but has always managed to restore its power.

¿Por qué volvió a suceder en 1991?

Why did it happen again in 1991?

¿Cómo es posible explicar el fracaso de la democracia en Rusia con Yeltsin y el resurgir de la dictadura bajo el liderazgo de Putin?

How is it possible to explain the failure of democracy in Russia with Yeltsin and the resurgence of the dictatorship under the leadership of Putin?

Expertos de toda clase han expresado puntos de vista muy diversos.

Experts of all kinds have expressed very diverse views.

Se han publicado muchísimos análisis sobre la cuestión y la literatura sigue creciendo a razón de varios volúmenes cada año, pero se caracteriza por ser estrecha de miras.

Many analyzes have been published on the issue and literature continues to grow at the rate of several volumes every year, but is characterized by being narrow of sights.

En gran parte se trata del punto de vista de los intelectuales liberales de Moscú y otras grandes ciudades, una casta reducida y aislada cuya influencia nunca ha sido tan importante como creían tanto quienes forman parte de ella como sus aliados en Occidente.

In large part it is the point of view of the liberal intellectuals of Moscow and other large cities, a reduced and isolated caste whose influence has never been as important as they believed both those who are part of it and their allies in the West.

Los analistas occidentales también se han centrado demasiado en presentar a Putin como la encarnación de la «cleptocracia» o del «Estado mafioso», descripciones de un sistema que en realidad es demasiado complejo como para poder ser explicado tan solo en función de una corrupción orientada al enriquecimiento personal o de las maquinaciones de un único hombre y su séquito oligárquico.

Western analysts have also focused too much on presenting Putin as the incarnation of "kleptocracy" or the "mafia state", descriptions of a system that is actually too complex to be able to be explained only based on a corruption oriented to personal enrichment or the machinations of a single man and its oligarchic entourage.

Además, en todo caso, gran parte de lo que se denomina el «sistema Putin» existía ya durante los años de Yeltsin.

In addition, in any case, much of what is called the "Putin System" already existed during the Yeltsin years.

Otro problema, aún mayor, que presenta este tipo de literatura es su ahistoricismo.

Another problem, even greater, that presents this type of literature is its ahistoricism.

Con demasiada frecuencia, la política rusa contemporánea se analiza sin tener el suficiente conocimiento sobre la historia rusa.

Too often, contemporary Russian policy is analyzed without having enough knowledge about Russian history.

Pero comprender el pasado del país es esencial para poder dar sentido a todo lo que ha sucedido en Rusia durante los últimos treinta años.

But understanding the country's past is essential to make sense of everything that has happened in Russia during the last thirty years.

La historia y el mito —y el uso que el régimen de Putin ha dado a ambos— deben tenerse en cuenta si queremos entender hacia dónde se dirige la historia de Rusia.

The story and myth - and the use that the Putin regime has given to both - must be taken into account if we want to understand where the story of Russia is directed.

Los acontecimientos de 1991 no fueron una revolución sino una abdicación del poder por parte del Partido Comunista.

The events of 1991 were not a revolution but an abdication of power by the Communist Party.

No se produjo un alzamiento masivo ni un movimiento de oposición que derrocara al régimen soviético en Rusia.

There was no massive uprising or an opposition movement that overthrew the Soviet regime in Russia.

No había partidos, sindicatos ni agrupaciones civiles preparados para tomar el poder, como sí ocurrió en las revoluciones de Europa del Este en 1989.

There were no matches, unions or civil groups prepared to take power, as it did in the revolutions of Eastern Europe in 1989.

La multitud que se congregó para defender la Casa Blanca del golpe no fue tan abultada como se afirmaría más tarde, cuando se dijo que hasta cuarenta mil personas habían permanecido de pie bajo la lluvia durante la noche del 20 al 21 de agosto para defender de los tanques al Gobierno de Yeltsin.

Those unnecessary deaths were enough to end the determination of the coup plotters, even within the KGB.

If that was a victory for democracy, for a long time it was not as it was perceived.

In 1994, according to the reliable survey service of Yuri Levada, only 7 percent of the Russian population thought that the fall of the Soviet regime was a democratic victory.

The rest saw it as a "power struggle" between leaders or a "tragedy that would have terrible effects" for the country.

Yeltsin filled his government as ancient communists and decided to recover the old Soviet institutions (the Army, the State Bank, the Soviet Union seat in the United Nations).

Approved in a hurried manner in 1992 by the young paladins of the free market of the Yeltsin team - Yegor Gaidar, the Prime Minister, and Anatoli Chubáis, VicePrimer Minister -, the program consisted of giving the population some coupons with which they could buy shares of the privatized state companies.

Debido a que el valor de aquellos no estaba vinculado a la inflación, los hombres de negocios que tuvieran acceso a préstamos de la banca estatal —a su vez encantada de facilitar grandes sumas a sus amigos en la compraventa— pudieron hacerse con empresas a cambio de auténticas miserias.

Because the value of those was not linked to inflation, businessmen who had access to loans from state banks - in turn delighted to facilitate great sums to their friends in sale - could be done with companies in exchange for authentic miseries.

Democracy (demokratiya) called her "shit" (Dermokratiya).
