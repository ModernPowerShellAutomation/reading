Determined to disassemble Catalina's policies, she revoked many of the freedoms she had granted to the aristocracy, reduced the elective component in the provincial governments and imposed a military order in society, for which she promulgated a series of decrees with the aim of ending any sign of moral laxity, from books, French music and fashion until the habit of socializing after ten o'clock at night. Accused of betrayal for his enemies, Speranski was dismissed from office and sent into exile in March 1812.

Three months later, the French imperial army, the great Armée, began the invasion of Russia.

Napoleon's objective was simple: forcing the Russians to fulfill the commitments acquired with France in the Tilsit Treaties (1807), signed after the defeat of the Russian forces at the hands of the French in the battle of Friedland.

More than half of Russia's exports were English acquisitions, raw materials in particular (wood, hemp, linen, wheat, potassium and wax), so the blockade, of being completely implemented, would have meant for Russia an economic and financial crisis.

But, as he understood, there was no incoherence: Russia was not yet mature enough to enjoy the freedom that corresponded to Europe.

Starting from the Duchy of Warsaw, a clientele state created by Napoleon in 1807, crossed the Neman River and entered the Polish territories of Russia.

Napoleon believed that he could put Russia on a single devastating victory like the one that, in 1805, had achieved in Austerlitz against Russians themselves and the Austrians themselves.

But, since then, Alejandro had learned that the only way to save his empire was not to combat Napoleon's forces in the open field, but to retire and attract them in the interior of Russia, where they would be defeated by winter frosts and supply problems that they thought to carry them through the tactics of the burned earth.

Determined to face face to face with the Russian army, Napoleon advanced to Moscow.

These, exhausted and demoralized, had believed that the fight would end when Moscow took, but once there they could not even find the enemy.

He contributed a great contingent to the coalition army that managed to defeat the great Armée in the crucial battle of Leipzig, in 1813, from where the French crossed the Rin to harsh.
