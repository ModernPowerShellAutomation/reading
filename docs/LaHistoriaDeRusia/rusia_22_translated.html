They suffered a very hard defeat against a Swedish force commanded by Carlos XII and formed only by nine thousand men, who were better trained and better equipped and had a greater artillery force.

The Russians launched a new campaign in 1701, the year in which the Swedes were distracted in another war against Poland.

Pedro's army also captured Narva and Dorpat (Tartu), conquests that he proposed to return to the Swedes if Carlos allowed him to maintain St. Petersburg.

Pedro consideraba que este último era el más importante de los puertos bálticos, porque desde el Nevá los barcos podían llegar hasta el río Volga y el mar Caspio, con lo que Rusia se convertiría en la principal ruta de tránsito entre Asia y Europa.

Pedro considered that the latter was the most important of the Baltic ports, because from the Nevá the ships could reach the Volga River and the Caspian Sea, with which Russia would become the main traffic route between Asia and Europe.

Carlos XII rejected Pedro's proposal.

His desire was to eliminate the threat of Russians for the Baltic lands of Sweden, on which the Swedes depended to obtain cereals.

Once the Poles were defeated, Carlos directed his armies against those, who were then in Lithuania.

Carlos's plan was to attack Moscow, dethrone the Tsar and divide Russia into small feuds that would be distributed among the Boyardos.
