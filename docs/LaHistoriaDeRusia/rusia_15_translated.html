At that time, the meaning of the word was closer to that of "imposing" and "formidable" than to the "cruel" or "hard", it was basically a positive use of the term.

These lands were delivered to the Opríchniki, a new class of faithful servers formed by about five thousand men, who made up the personal army of Iván IV and whose mission was to combat internal sedition.

The Opríchniki recruited them on the only basis of their loyalty to the tsar and were forbidden to maintain contact with the nobles of the Zemshchina, the lands that were outside the opríchnin.
