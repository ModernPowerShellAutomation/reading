The duel they manifested was a natural response to fear and confusion that, by force, had to feel before their death.

Beria was the dominant figure, but high military and party positions opposed her program, which involved the dismantling of the gulag and a relaxation of Soviet policies in western Ukraine, the Baltic region and Eastern Germany.

On June 26, Beria was arrested in a blow to the Kremlin orchestrated by Jrushchov and high commanders of the army.

He was judged in secret and then was shot.

Nikita Jruschov salió de aquel golpe como cabeza de la dirección conjunta.

Nikita Jrushchov came out of that blow as the head of the joint direction.

Personaje ampuloso y de carácter tempestuoso, con unos humildes orígenes campesinos, había ascendido de rango actuando como leal verdugo de las políticas de Stalin.

Ampular and stormy character, with humble peasant origins, had risen of rank acting as loyal executioner of Stalin's policies.

Estuvo muy implicado en la represión de la década de 1930, primero como dirigente del Partido en Moscú y después en Ucrania, donde fue responsable de la detención de un cuarto de millón de ciudadanos.

He was very involved in the repression of the 1930s, first as leader of the party in Moscow and then in Ukraine, where he was responsible for the arrest of a quarter of a million citizens.

Quizá fuera el sentimiento de culpa lo que le llevó a exponer los crímenes del reinado de Stalin en su «discurso secreto» de 1956.

Perhaps it was the feeling of guilt that led him to expose the crimes of Stalin's reign in his "secret speech" of 1956.

La población del Gulag había alcanzado su cifra máxima en 1952, cuando en las colonias y campos de trabajo llegó a haber en torno a dos millones de presos.

The population of the Gulag had reached its maximum figure in 1952, when there were about two million prisoners in the colonies and work fields.

Tras la muerte de Stalin, los prisioneros comenzaron a ser liberados, primero los que tenían sentencias más cortas —sobre todo delincuentes— y después los «políticos», cuyos casos fueron revisados por la Prokuratura soviética, un proceso largo y complicado que se veía continuamente obstruido por unas autoridades renuentes a rendir cuentas por sus errores.

After Stalin's death, the prisoners began to be released, first those who had shorter sentences - especially criminals - and then the "politicians", whose cases were reviewed by the Soviet prokurac, a long and complicated process that was continually obstructed by some renewing authorities to account for their mistakes.

Con el regreso de todos aquellos presos, el régimen tenía que explicar lo que había sucedido.

With the return of all those prisoners, the regime had to explain what had happened.

Pero ¿qué porcentaje de la verdad podía revelarse?

But what percentage of the truth could be revealed?

All leaders feared what could happen if the full scope of that terror came to light.

¿Se les pedirían cuentas a ellos?

Would they be asked for accounts?

¿No desearía la gente saber por qué no habían detenido aquellos arrestos masivos?

Wouldn't people want to know why they had not stopped those mass arrests?

Se formó una comisión para investigar la represión sufrida por los miembros del Partido entre 1935 y 1940.

A commission was formed to investigate the repression suffered by party members between 1935 and 1940.

El politburó quedó tan conmocionado por sus hallazgos que decidió exponerlos (aunque no todo el alcance del terror que había sufrido el país en su conjunto) en el XX Congreso del Partido, el primero que tuvo lugar después de la muerte de Stalin, en febrero de 1956.

The Politburó was so shocked by his findings that he decided to expose them (although not all the scope of the terror that the country had suffered as a whole) in the XX Congress of the Party, the first that took place after the death of Stalin, in February 1956.

El texto se preparó colectivamente y fue leído por Jruschov.

The text prepared collectively and was read by Jrushchov.

El discurso explicaba los detalles de las purgas del Partido y de los garrafales errores de Stalin durante la guerra, y los atribuía tanto al hecho de que el dictador se había desviado de los principios leninistas como al «culto a la personalidad», que había imposibilitado oponer resistencia a sus políticas.

The speech explained the details of the purges of the party and the carafales Stalin errors during the war, and attributed them both to the fact that the dictator had deviated from the Leninist principles and the "cult of personality", which had impossible to oppose resistance to their policies.

Jruschov subrayó que la nueva dirección acababa de enterarse de todos los detalles revelados por la comisión, y con ello trató de absolverla y de transferir la culpa a Stalin.

Jrushchov stressed that the new direction had just learned of all the details revealed by the Commission, and with it tried to acquit it and transfer the guilt to Stalin.

La posible culpa que tuviera la dirección del Partido ni se mencionó.

The possible fault that the party's leadership had or mentioned.

El objetivo del discurso era restablecer la fe en la organización, al presentar el estalinismo como una aberración de los ideales socialistas de la Revolución de Octubre.

The objective of the discourse was to restore faith in the organization, by presenting Stalinism as an aberration of the socialist ideals of the October Revolution.

Jruschov terminó pidiendo que sus revelaciones no se comunicaran más allá del Partido.

Jrushchov ended up asking that his revelations would not communicate beyond the party.

Sin embargo, el texto de su discurso se leyó ante millones de sus miembros en reuniones celebradas en toda la Unión Soviética y se envió a los gobiernos comunistas de Europa del Este.

However, the text of his speech was read before millions of its members in meetings held throughout the Soviet Union and sent to the communist governments of Eastern Europe.

En Polonia fue publicado y salió impreso en The New York Times, y desde Occidente se filtró de vuelta a la Unión Soviética.

In Poland it was published and was printed in The New York Times, and from the West he leaked back to the Soviet Union.

En el clima de «deshielo» de la época de Jruschov, momento en que la censura estaba experimentando un relajamiento gradual, el texto impulsó una evaluación más general del historial del Partido por parte de la intelectualidad soviética.

In the "thaw" climate of the Jrushchov era, at which time censorship was experiencing a gradual relaxation, the text promoted a more general evaluation of the history of the party by the Soviet intellectuality.

«El congreso puso fin a nuestro solitario cuestionamiento del sistema soviético —contó Liudmila Alexéieva, que más tarde se convertiría en una conocida disidente, pero que en ese momento era estudiante en la Universidad de Moscú—.

"Congress ended our lonely questioning of the Soviet system," Alexéieva, who would later become a well -known dissident, had a student at the University of Moscow.

Chicos y chicas jóvenes empezaron a perder el miedo a compartir opiniones, información, creencias, preguntas.

Young guys and girls began to lose their fear of sharing opinions, information, beliefs, questions.

Todas las noches nos reuníamos en pisos abarrotados para recitar poesía, leer textos “no oficiales” y contarnos historias que, tomadas en conjunto, daban una imagen realista de lo que estaba pasando en nuestro país».

Every night we met in crowded floors to recite poetry, read "unofficial" texts and tell us stories that, taken together, gave a realistic image of what was happening in our country.

For the first time in his story he admitted that he was wrong, and not for a minor error, but catastrophic, and that he had been lying over it all the time.

¿Cómo podría recuperar su credibilidad?

How could your credibility recover?

El Deshielo había empezado en el ámbito de la literatura, que durante toda la historia rusa moderna ha sido un sustituto de la política.

The thaw had begun in the field of literature, which throughout Russian history has been a substitute for politics.

Una vez retirado el yugo del conformismo estalinista, los escritores se aplicaron a retratar la vida soviética con mayor sinceridad.

Once the yoke from Stalinist conformism was removed, writers applied to portray Soviet life with greater sincerity.

La novela El deshielo (1954), de Iliá Ehrenburg —que dio nombre a este periodo—, cuenta la historia de una mujer oprimida por su marido, un déspota director de fábrica, que encuentra finalmente el valor para dejarlo durante el deshielo de primavera.

The novel El Tello (1954), by Iliá Ehrenburg - who gave name to this period - tells the story of a woman oppressed by her husband, a despota factory director, who finally finds the value to leave him during the spring mel.

El punto álgido de este renacimiento literario llegó en 1962, con la publicación de Un día en la vida de Iván Denísovich, de Alexánder Solzhenitsyn, la primera novela que trataba de los campos de trabajo de Stalin, en la revista Novy Mir («Nuevo Mundo»).

The high point of this literary rebirth arrived in 1962, with the publication of one day in the life of Iván Denívich, by Alexánder Solzhenitsyn, the first novel that was about the work fields of Stalin, in the magazine Noval Mir ("New World").

Tuvo millones de lectores.

He had millions of readers.

But, although Gulag's conviction was allowed, the October revolution was out of any question.

El doctor Zhivago, la épica novela de Borís Pasternak, ambientada en el contexto de la revolución y la guerra civil, fue rechazada en 1956 por diversas revistas, incluida la progresista Novy Mir, por ser «antisoviética».

Dr. Zhivago, Borís Pasternak's epic novel, set in the context of the revolution and civil war, was rejected in 1956 by various magazines, including the progressive Noval Mir, for being "antisovietic."

La novela llegó clandestinamente a Milán, donde se publicó en italiano, y pronto se convirtió en un éxito de ventas internacional.

The novel arrived clandestinely to Milan, where it was published in Italian, and soon became an international sales success.

Pasternak fue propuesto para el Premio Nobel en 1958, pero se vio obligado a rechazarlo por la presión del Gobierno soviético.

Pasternak was proposed for the Nobel Prize in 1958, but was forced to reject it for the pressure of the Soviet government.

El Deshielo supuso la apertura parcial de la sociedad soviética a Occidente.

The thaw meant the partial opening of the Soviet society to the West.

Por primera vez llegaron grupos de turistas extranjeros.

For the first time there were groups of foreign tourists.

El Gobierno de Jruschov veía el turismo como una forma de ingresar dólares al tiempo que mostraba los logros soviéticos al mundo (acababa de lanzarse el programa Sputnik de exploración espacial).

The Jrushchov Government saw tourism as a way of entering dollars while showing Soviet achievements to the world (the Sputnik space exploration program had just launched).

In 1957, Moscow welcomed the World Youth Festival.

The objective of Kremlin was to win the young people of capitalist countries, seduce them with the Soviet lifestyle.

But the result was the opposite.

Con sus pantalones vaqueros y su actitud desenfadada, los visitantes convirtieron a la juventud soviética al estilo de vida occidental.

With their jeans and their casual attitude, visitors made Soviet youth in the western lifestyle.

El rock and roll y sus modas capturaron la imaginación de una generación de estudiantes soviéticos demasiado sofisticados para la cultura aburrida y conformista del Komsomol, las Juventudes Comunistas.

Rock and Roll and its fashions captured the imagination of a generation of Soviet students too sophisticated for the boring and conformist culture of the Komsomol, the communist youth.

En sus radios de onda corta escuchaban Voice of America y Radio Free Europe, donde se combinaban el rock y el jazz con las noticias y la información sobre las libertades occidentales.

In their short wave radios they listened to Voice of America and Radio Free Europe, where rock and jazz were combined with the news and information about Western freedoms.

Aquella generación formó su imagen de Occidente a través de las películas de Hollywood, que se exhibían en los cines soviéticos como parte del Deshielo y que tuvieron una influencia gigantesca.

That generation formed its image of the West through the Hollywood films, which were exhibited in Soviet cinemas as part of the thaw and that had a gigantic influence.

Según el poeta Joseph Brodsky, las películas de Tarzán «hicieron más por la desestalinización que todos los discursos que diera Jruschov en el XX Congreso del Partido y después».

According to the poet Joseph Brodsky, Tarzan's films "made more for the deestalination than all the speeches that Jrushchov gave in the XX Congress of the Party and then."

Jrushchov tried to do it through Komsomol.

In the Virgin Land campaign of 1954-1963, tens of thousands of boys and young girls of the Komsomol were offered volunteers to work on the new collective farms of the virgin steppes of Kazakhstan.

Jrushchov was dismissed in 1964.

The political reforms of Jrushchov had raised the opposition of the regional leaders of the party, since they weakened their economic power and forced them to submit to the periodic elections of the Central Committee, an agency that with Stalin had occupied as a kind of natural law, which had allowed them to build their own client networks in the provinces.

The erratic leadership of Jrushchov, his tendency to act by intuition and then attack his critics, his interference on issues on which he lacked knowledge and his dangerous confrontation with the United States in the missile crisis in Cuba made him lose the support of those among his party classmates who wanted a more stable and collective style of government.
