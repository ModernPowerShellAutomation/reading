Tras reprimir un segundo levantamiento de Tver, en 1327, Kalitá fue ascendido por Uzbeg al título de gran príncipe de Vladímir, el poder ruso de más alto rango que podía ostentarse en las antiguas tierras de la Rus de Kiev.

After suppressing a second Tver uprising, in 1327, Kalitá was promoted by Uzbeg to the title of Great Prince of Vladimir, the highest Russian power that could be held in the old lands of the Rus of kyiv.

Cuando, en 1339, Kalitá logró sofocar un levantamiento en Smolensk, el kan lo recompensó sancionando legalmente su testamento y confiriendo la sucesión de sus hijos en el trono de Moscú.

When, in 1339, Kalitá managed to quell an uprising in Smolensk, the Kan rewarded him legally sanctioning his will and conferring the succession of his children on the throne of Moscow.

[53] En adelante, el cargo de gran príncipe de Vladímir lo ostentaron los Daniílovich, viniendo así a incorporarse al resto de las potestades que mantenía sobre Rusia el gran príncipe de Moscovia.

[53] From now on, the position of Great Prince of Vladimir was held by Danilovich, thus coming to join the rest of the powers that the great Prince of Moscow maintained over Russia.

El creciente temor que sentían los mongoles ante Lituania fue también un importante factor de su apoyo a Moscú.

The growing fear that Mongols felt before Lithuania was also an important factor of their support for Moscow.

A principios del siglo XIV, los lituanos estaban expandiendo de forma incesante su control sobre las antiguas tierras occidentales de la Rus de Kiev.

At the beginning of the fourteenth century, the Lithuanians were incessantly expanding their control over the ancient Western lands of the kyiv Rus.

When they could not annexed them by pressure or persuasion, the Lithuanians resorted to religion.

They established their own metropolitan archbishop to differentiate the western territories from the rest of the Orthodox Russia.

En la década de 1330, Smolensk, Nóvgorod y Tver estaban muy cerca de unir su suerte a la de Lituania; Moscú intentaba controlarlas mediante amenazas militares, y los mongoles, por su parte, se veían ante el peligro de que, en su frontera occidental, apareciera un nuevo Estado con el poder suficiente como para socavar su dominio sobre las tierras rusas.

In the 1330s, Smolensk, Nóvgorod and Tver were very close to joining their fate to Lithuania; Moscow tried to control them through military threats, and the Mongols, meanwhile, were seen before the danger that, on their western border, a new state appeared with sufficient power to undermine their dominion over the Russian lands.

La mejor política del kan fue aumentar el poder de Moscú para que opusiera resistencia a los lituanos, una estrategia que demostró tener éxito.

The best policy of Kan was to increase Moscow's power to oppose resistance to Lithuanians, a strategy that showed success.

Nóvgorod fue devuelto al redil de Moscú; Tver se desintegró en diversos feudos boyardos enfrentados en guerras, y Smolensk fue anexionado por Moscú en 1352.

Nóvgorod was returned to Moscow's fold; Tver disintegrated into various Boyardos fiefs confronted in wars, and Smolensk was annexed by Moscow in 1352.

A medida que el poder de Moscú crecía, los mongoles intentaron contenerlo, recurriendo de nuevo a la estrategia del «divide y vencerás».

As Moscow's power grew, the Mongols tried to contain it, again resorting to the strategy of "divide and win."

En el oeste alentaron su crecimiento como un Estado tapón frente a Polonia y Lituania, pero en el norte y el este, donde el control de los mongoles dependía de la debilidad de Moscú, incitaron a los elementos antimoscotivas.

In the west they encouraged their growth as a state cap against Poland and Lithuania, but in the north and the east, where the control of the Mongols depended on the weakness of Moscow, they incited the anti -statements.

Sin embargo, en la segunda mitad del siglo XIII, era ya demasiado tarde para que la Horda de Oro pudiera contener la ascensión de Moscú.

However, in the second half of the thirteenth century, it was already too late for the Golden Horde to contain the ascent of Moscow.

La ciudad se había convertido en un importante centro comercial, gracias en gran parte a la protección de los mongoles y al fomento de las rutas comerciales internacionales a través de las vías fluviales de Moscú.

The city had become an important shopping center, thanks in large part to the protection of Mongols and the promotion of international trade routes through the river roads in Moscow.

Tan solo el comercio europeo que pasaba por Nóvgorod aportaba una gran riqueza a Moscovia, que se quedaba una buena parte de los impuestos que recaudaba para la Horda de Oro.

Only the European trade that passed through Nóvgorod contributed a great wealth to Moscow, which remained a good part of the taxes that raised for the Golden Horde.

Surgieron nuevas ciudades, se construyeron grandes iglesias de piedra y la artesanía y las industrias de Moscú experimentaron un renacimiento.

New cities emerged, large stone churches and crafts and Moscow industries were built experienced a rebirth.

Las primeras murallas e iglesias de piedra del Kremlin de la ciudad se construyeron durante esa época; en el caso de los muros, por orden del gran príncipe Dmitri en 1366-1367.

The first walls and stone churches of the city's Kremlin were built during that time; In the case of the walls, by order of the great prince Dmitri in 1366-1367.

Unos equipos colosales, mucho mayores que la mano de obra que cualquier gobernante europeo hubiera sido capaz de reunir para un proyecto de este tipo, llevaron hasta allí más de cincuenta mil metros cúbicos de piedra desde canteras lejanas.

Colossal equipment, much greater than the labor that any European ruler would have been able to gather for such a project, took there more than fifty thousand cubic meters of stone from distant quarries.

La construcción del Kremlin era un símbolo del poder de Moscú.

The construction of the Kremlin was a symbol of the power of Moscow.

También fue el resultado de un nuevo acuerdo con la Iglesia, que se alió con la causa de Moscú como centro de poder nacional para liberar a los ortodoxos del dominio mongol.

It was also the result of a new agreement with the Church, which allied with the cause of Moscow as a center of national power to free the orthodox from the Mongol domain.

La alianza había comenzado en 1325, cuando Pedro II, metropolitano de Kiev y toda la Rus, trasladó su sede desde Vladímir a Moscú por petición de Iván Kalitá.

The Alliance had begun in 1325, when Pedro II, Metropolitano of kyiv and the entire Rus, moved its headquarters from Vladimir to Moscow at the request of Iván Kalitá.

Para señalar su llegada, Iván ordenó rápidamente la construcción de la catedral de la Dormición en el Kremlin, la primera iglesia de piedra dentro de sus murallas.

To point out his arrival, Iván quickly ordered the construction of the bedroom cathedral in the Kremlin, the first stone church within its walls.

Desde ese momento, Iván dotó a la Iglesia de grandes latifundios.

From that moment, Iván gave the church with great estates.

Those lands became so extensive that a contract was written to define the obligations of the Metropolitan towards the ruling dynasty.

En términos prácticos, confirmaba su posición como vasallo del gran príncipe.

In practical terms, he confirmed his position as the vassal of the great prince.

[54] The Moscow Alliance with the Church was consolidated by the military defeat that inflicted on a large Tartar army in 1380.

Era la primera vez que una fuerza rusa lograba obtener una victoria contra los mongoles.

It was the first time that a Russian force managed to obtain a victory against the Mongols.

El conflicto estuvo originado por las demandas de la Horda de Oro para que Moscú le pagara tributos.

The conflict was caused by the demands of the Golden Horde for Moscow to pay taxes.

Mamai Kan had told that the great prince Dmitri would send him some increasing income.

But, at that time, Moscow's revenues were being resented due to a commercial dispute between Nóvgorod and its western partners.

Las cosas llegaron a un punto crítico en 1378, cuando Sarái fue atacado por un kan mongol rival, lo que obligó a Mamái a reforzar su ejército, en aquel momento diezmado por la peste, y exigir a Moscú un pago adicional.

Things reached a critical point in 1378, when Sarái was attacked by a rival Kan Mongol, which forced Mamai to reinforce his army, at that time decimated by the plague, and demand from Moscow an additional payment.

A Mongolian force formed by thirty thousand men was sent to collect the required amount.

Dmitri reunió un ejército del mismo tamaño, marchó hacia el sur para encontrarse con los mongoles y los derrotó en Kulikovo, cerca del río Don.

Dmitri gathered an army of the same size, marched south to meet the Mongols and defeated them in Kulikovo, near the Don River.

La victoria le valió su apodo, Dmitri Donskói, que significa «del Don».

The victory earned him his nickname, Dmitri Donskói, which means "of the gift."

[55] Para celebrar la victoria, la Iglesia de Moscú introdujo una festividad anual en el día de San Demetrio, el santo patrón del gran príncipe.

[55] To celebrate the victory, the Moscow church introduced an annual feast on the day of San Demetrio, the patron saint of the great prince.

A partir de ese momento, Kulikovo empezó a aparecer en los relatos de la historia nacional como el principio de la liberación de Rusia de los infieles tártaros y, después, como el momento de su «despertar nacional».

From that moment on, Kulikovo began to appear in the stories of national history as the principle of Russia's liberation from the infidels of Tartars and then, as the moment of his "national awakening."

Kulikovo sigue celebrándose aún en Rusia.

Kulikovo is still being held in Russia.

Putin ha hecho con frecuencia referencia a ella como prueba de que, en el siglo XIV, Rusia era ya una gran potencia, la salvadora de Europa frente a la amenaza mongola.

Putin has frequently referred to her as proof that, in the fourteenth century, Russia was already a great power, the savior of Europe in front of the Mongolian threat.

Esta idea de Rusia como la guardiana que protege a Europa de las «hordas asiáticas» se convirtió en parte de la mitología nacional a partir del siglo XVI, cuando Moscovia comenzó a mirarse a sí misma como una potencia europea en la estepa asiática.

This idea of Russia as the Guardian that protects Europe from "Asian hordes" became part of national mythology from the 16th century, when Moscow began to look at herself as a European power in the Asian steppe.

La idea alcanzó su apogeo con los poetas «escitas» de principios del siglo XX, entre ellos Alexánder Blok, quien reprochó a la ingrata Europa la falta de reconocimiento del hecho de que Rusia la había protegido de las tribus asiáticas: Como esclavos, obedientes y aborrecidos, fuimos el escudo entre las razas de Europa y la rugiente horda mongola.

The idea reached its peak with the "Scythian" poets of the early twentieth century, including Alexánder Blok, who reproached the ungrateful Europe for the lack of recognition of the fact that Russia had protected her from Asian tribes: As slaves, obedient and abhorred, We were the shield between the races from Europe and the roaring Mongola.

[56] Today, Kulikovo's victory has been linked in the nationalist consciousness to other historical episodes in which, despite the fact that Russia's military sacrifice allegedly saved the West-for example, in 1812-1815 (against Napoleon) or in 1941-1945-, this was not grateful or recognized by his western allies.

El profundo rencor hacia Occidente que se siente en el país hunde sus raíces en este mito nacional.

Despite his victory, Moscow suffered huge losses.

Two years later, his forces were too exhausted to be able to defend themselves when Toqtamish, the new Kan, sent his army in revenge.

After three days of clashes, the Mongols looted the city and killed half of the population.

Aware of their helplessness, the Russian princes rushed to go to Sarái to swear submission to the Golden Horde.

At the same time they dealt with Moscow's challenge, the Mongols were facing a new threat from the empire that was then emerging in Central Asia, under the command of Timur, better known as Tamerlán.

Timur's army had conquered Persia and the Caucasus and then prepared to destroy the main commercial bases of the Golden Horde, which began a slow but definitive decline.
