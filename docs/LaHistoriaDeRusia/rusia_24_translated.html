Los eslavófilos sostenían que había puesto a Rusia en el camino de una vía falsa; la de imitar a Occidente e importar su cultura materialista, que solo podía lograrse a expensas del carácter nacional, con la pérdida de los valores espirituales y las tradiciones que distinguían a Rusia de Europa.

The Slavophiles argued that he had put Russia on the path of a false route; that of imitating the West and importing its materialistic culture, which could only be achieved at the expense of the national character, with the loss of spiritual values and traditions that distinguished Russia from Europe.

Pero para la intelectualidad occidentalizante, que miraba hacia Europa en busca de sus valores e ideales, lo que Pedro había hecho era fundar una nueva patria, la Rusia petrina, la única en la que ellos podían creer.

But for the westernizing intellectuality, which looked towards Europe in search of its values and ideals, what Pedro had done was to found a new homeland, the Petrina Russia, the only one in which they could believe.

Su posición la expresó el filósofo del siglo XIX Vladímir Soloviov, que afirmaba que Pedro había salvado a Rusia de desarrollar un carácter «puramente asiático»: «Todo lo que en la esfera del pensamiento y la creatividad hemos tenido de bueno y original surgió únicamente como resultado de las reformas petrinas; sin ellas no tendríamos ni a Pushkin, ni a Glinka, ni a Gógol ni a Dostoievski, ni a Turguénev ni a Tolstói».

His position was expressed by the nineteenth -century philosopher Vladimir Soloviov, who claimed that Pedro had saved Russia to develop a "purely Asian" character: "Everything that in the sphere of thought and creativity we have had good and original arose only as a result of the Petrinas reforms; Without them we would not have Pushkin, either Glinka, neither Gogol nor Dostoevski, nor Turguénev nor Tolstoy.

[130] Una cosa sí que puede afirmarse con certeza, y es que aquellas reformas crearon una profunda brecha cultural entre la civilización urbana de las élites occidentalizadas y el mundo rural de los campesinos, privados de educación y de libertad, consumidos por la servidumbre y la pobreza, que se aferraban a sus anticuadas tradiciones comunales e interpretaban el universo por medio de supersticiones paganas y creencias ortodoxas.

[130] One thing can be affirmed with certainty, and that those reforms created a deep cultural gap between the urban civilization of Westernized elites and the rural world of peasants, deprived of education and freedom, consumed by servitude and poverty, which clung to their outdated communal traditions and interpreted the universe through pagan superstitions and orthodox beliefs.

Esa brecha permaneció abierta hasta 1917 y fue la línea divisoria sobre la que se libraría la revolución.

That gap remained open until 1917 and was the dividing line on which the revolution would be fought.

Los bolcheviques se inspiraron en Pedro el Grande (el poeta Max Voloshin lo llamaría «el primer bolchevique»).

The Bolsheviks were inspired by Pedro el Grande (the poet Max Voloshin would call him "the first Bolshevik").

Al obligar a la atrasada Rusia a ponerse a la par con Occidente, había ofrecido un ejemplo para el programa de hipermodernización forzosa de los propios bolcheviques.

By forcing the backward Russia to get along with the West, he had offered an example for the forced hypermodernization program of the Bolsheviks themselves.

Pero los métodos coercitivos de Pedro tuvieron, en muchos aspectos, un efecto contrario al que él pretendía.

But Pedro's coercive methods had, in many ways, an opposite effect to the one he intended.

En vez de modernizar el país, reforzaron la tiranía estatista y las costumbres serviles del sistema de servidumbre que hasta entonces habían mantenido a Rusia, como así continuarían haciéndolo, en un estado relativamente atrasado en comparación con Occidente, donde las sociedades gozaban de mayor libertad.

Instead of modernizing the country, they reinforced statist tyranny and servile customs of the servitude system that until then had maintained Russia, as they would continue to do so, in a relatively backward state compared to the West, where societies enjoyed greater freedom.

Ahí residía la paradoja, no solo de su proyecto de reforma, sino de todos aquellos que, en lo posterior, estuvieron a la cabeza de gobiernos rusos de tendencia occidentalizadora.

There was the paradox, not only of its reform project, but of all those who, in the later, were at the head of Russian governments of westernizing tendency.

En ausencia de una sociedad libre o de una esfera pública activa disponible para la empresa, era el propio Estado el que actuaba como motor único de la reforma.

In the absence of a free society or an active public sphere available for the company, it was the state itself that acted as the single engine of the reform.

Y forzar los cambios desde arriba solo podía abrir una brecha aún más profunda entre el Estado y el pueblo, reforzando la coerción estatal como mecanismo principal de la reforma.

And forcing the changes from above could only open an even deeper gap between the State and the people, reinforcing state coercion as the main mechanism of the reform.

En 1722, solo tres años antes de su muerte, Pedro anunció una nueva Ley de Sucesión.

In 1722, only three years before his death, Pedro announced a new succession law.

No tenía ningún heredero varón.

He had no male heir.

His first child, Alexéi, had been executed in his order four years before, accused of betrayal, after having fled his father's harassment to take refuge in Vienna.

Pedro reserved the right to appoint his heir (a very clear statement that he was above the law).

Su elección recayó en su segunda esposa, una mujer de humildes orígenes polacos, a la que, en 1724, coronó como emperatriz Catalina I en la catedral de la Dormición.

His choice fell to his second wife, a woman of humble Polish origins, to whom, in 1724, he crowned as Empress Catalina I in the cathedral of the sleeping.

En la ceremonia de coronación, Pedro sustituyó al patriarca como oficiante de la bendición divina, una alteración simbólica más de la antigua symphonia bizantina entre Iglesia y Estado, que habían aparecido como socios iguales en los rituales de coronación ejecutados con anterioridad.

In the coronation ceremony, Pedro replaced the patriarch as officiant of the divine blessing, a symbolic alteration of the former Byzantine Symphonia between Church and State, which had appeared as equal partners in the coronation rituals executed previously.

La voluntad del zar había llegado a tal punto que él mismo se encargaba de decidir el destino de Rusia y otorgar un estatus sagrado a su sucesor.

The will of the Tsar had reached such an extent that he himself was responsible for deciding the fate of Russia and granting a sacred status to his successor.

Dado lo divisivo que había resultado el reinado de Pedro el Grande, quizá sorprenda que su muerte no diera lugar a una guerra civil, como había ocurrido tras el fallecimiento de Iván el Terrible.

Given how divisive Pedro el Grande's reign had resulted, perhaps surprising that his death did not give rise to a civil war, as had happened after the death of Iván the terrible.

Las mujeres gobernantes fueron consideradas «humanas» y «sabias», de trato más suave y transigente que el dominante Pedro, lo que amplió el espacio para la política cortesana y contribuyó a estabilizar el sistema.

The ruling women were considered "human" and "wise," of softer and more compromising treatment than the dominant Pedro, which expanded the space for courtly politics and contributed to stabilize the system.

Las principales familias de la corte se agruparon en facciones, y esto dio a las gobernantes la oportunidad de afirmar su dominio por medio del «divide y vencerás», estrategia que Isabel y Catalina la Grande llegaron a manejar a la perfección.

The main families of the Court were grouped into features, and this gave the rulers the opportunity to affirm their domain through "divide and win", a strategy that Isabel and Catalina la Grande came to handle perfectly.

[132] The problems they faced to consolidate their monarchical authority had less to do with the genre than with nationality.

A excepción de Isabel, hija de Pedro, el resto de los gobernantes que, tras su muerte, tuvo Rusia en el siglo XVIII, o bien habían nacido en el extranjero (Catalina I, Pedro III y Catalina la Grande), o bien tenían padres alemanes (Pedro II e Iván VI), o bien se habían casado con miembros de familias alemanas (Ana I o Pablo I).

With the exception of Isabel, the daughter of Pedro, the rest of the rulers who, after his death, had Russia in the 18th century, or were born abroad (Catalina I, Pedro III and Catalina the Great), or had German parents (Pedro II and Iván VI), or had married members of German families (Ana I or Paul I).

En las dos ocasiones en las que se produjo un golpe palaciego para tomar el trono, la motivación de los conspiradores tenía que ver con la hostilidad contra los gobernantes extranjeros o contra la pleitesía a las potencias extranjeras a expensas de los intereses de Rusia.

On the two occasions in which there was a palatial blow to take the throne, the motivation of the conspirators had to do with the hostility against foreign rulers or against pleites to foreign powers at the expense of Russia's interests.

El primero de ellos se produjo tras la muerte de Ana I, en octubre de 1740.

The first of them occurred after the death of Ana I, in October 1740.

Ana era hija de Iván, hermanastro de Pedro el Grande.

Ana was the daughter of Iván, stepbrother of Pedro el Grande.

Antes de convertirse en emperatriz, había sido duquesa de Curlandia (parte de la actual Letonia).

Before becoming Empress, he had been a duchess of Curlandia (part of the current Latvia).

En Rusia había nutrido su Gobierno y sus regimientos de élite con miembros alemanes (de las familias Ostermann, Münnich y Lieven), cuyo indisimulado desprecio por los rusos inflamó el sentimiento nacionalista.

In Russia, he had nurtured his government and elite regiments with German members (of the Ostermann, Münnich and Lieven) families, whose indispecked contempt for the Russians inflamed the nationalist feeling.

La figura más odiada era su amante, Ernst Johann Bühren (Biron para los rusos), un alemán de Curlandia que, sin ocupar ningún puesto oficial, parecía ejercer un enorme poder.

The most hated figure was his lover, Ernst Johann Bühren (Biron for the Russians), a German from Curlandia who, without occupying any official position, seemed to exercise enormous power.

Al principio, el descontento con Biron estuvo limitado a pequeños grupos de oficiales a los que unía su deseo de tener un Gobierno ruso.

At first, the discontent with Biron was limited to small groups of officers who linked his desire to have a Russian government.

Pero se generalizó cuando aquel impuso la recaudación de una serie de impuestos atrasados que en realidad habían sido anulados por gobernantes anteriores.

But it was generalized when that imposed the collection of a series of backward taxes that had actually been annulled by previous rulers.

Cuando, a la muerte de Ana, Biron fue nombrado regente de su heredero, Iván VI, hijo pequeño del duque de Braunschweig-Mecklenburg, la oposición rusa, que entonces contaba ya con el regimiento Preobrazhenski como brazo fuerte, tomó el poder, arrestó a Biron y puso en el trono a Isabel I.

When, at Ana's death, Biron was appointed regent of her heir, Iván VI, young son of the Duke of Braunschweig-Mecklenburg, the Russian opposition, which then had the preobrazhenski regiment as a strong arm, took power, arrested Biron and put Isabel I on the throne.

Biron y el resto de los alemanes de su círculo fueron enviados a Siberia, mientras que los soldados rusos ejecutaron a los oficiales alemanes.

Biron and the rest of the Germans from his circle were sent to Siberia, while Russian soldiers executed German officers.

El segundo golpe antiextranjero lo llevó a cabo Catalina la Grande (ella misma extranjera) contra Pedro III, su propio esposo, en 1762.

The second anti -exponent blow was carried out by Catalina La Grande (herself) against Pedro III, her own husband, in 1762.

Catalina, nacida princesa Sofía de Anhalt-Zerbst, un Estado germano de carácter menor, había llegado a Rusia a los diecisiete años como futura esposa de su primo segundo, Carlos Pedro Uldarico de Schleswig-Holstein-Gottorp, nieto de Pedro el Grande y heredero de la emperatriz Isabel.

Catalina, born Princess Sofía de Anhalt-Zerbst, a minor German state, had arrived in Russia at seventeen as the future wife of her second cousin, Carlos Pedro Uldarico of Schleswig-Holstein-Gotorp, grandson of Pedro el Grande and heir of Empress Isabel.

En su boda, en 1745, Catalina se convirtió a la ortodoxia, abandonando su fe luterana, y tomó el nombre elegido para ella por Isabel, cuya madre había sido Catalina I.

At her wedding, in 1745, Catalina became orthodoxy, abandoning her Lutheran faith, and took the name chosen for her by Isabel, whose mother had been Catalina I.

El matrimonio fue un desastre.

In his memoirs he describes him as a stupid, cruel and petty man - an outrage that historians have questioned - and states that he was helpless.

The unhappy couple did not take long to wear separate lives.

Catalina began to have a relationship with Serguéi Saltykov, a handsome chambelán, and suffered two spontaneous abortions before giving birth to a son, Paul, the heir to the throne, in 1754.

It is not known if Saltykov was Paul's father, but that hints Catalina's memoirs, for the horror of her descendants, who, throughout the nineteenth century, censored all mention of her name.

Pedro III was a admirer of Federico el Grande, King of Prussia.

He left the alliance with Austria and France, on whose side he had been fighting Russia since the beginning of the seven -year war, and went to the Prussian side.

Its internal policy offended the Russian national feeling, in particular the land seizures of the Church, the military recruitment of the children of the priests and the order to withdraw from the orthodox churches all the icons except those of the Savior and the Mother of God, measures that showed their contempt for faith and aroused fears that it would house the intention of replacing it with luteranism.

Catalina justified her coup d'etat alleging that she was defending the orthodoxy of "destruction of their traditions."

Officially, it was reported that he had died of "hemorrhoidal colic", which led to a French philosopher to comment with humor that hemorrhoids should be very dangerous in Russia.
