If, in the treatment of the United States to Germany, the balance was increasingly inclined towards "peace," this was largely due to the progressive deterioration of relations between the United States and the Soviet Union.

Among a small circle of Washington observers with access to first -hand information, it was evident from the beginning that the incompatibility between Soviet and Western interests would lead to a conflict and, therefore, the clear delimitation of some areas of being able to constitute a prudent solution to the problems of the postwar period.

This was George Kennan's point of view that, on January 26, 1945, he wrote: «Why could we not reach a reasonable and definitive commitment with [the USSR]?

Six weeks later, Averell Harriman, the United States ambassador to Moscow, raised President Roosevelt with an answer to the actions of the Soviet Union in Eastern Europe much more pessimistic and implicitly contentious in his report: «Unless we are willing to accept a barbaric invasion of Europe in the middle Soviet domination policy […].

Harriman and Kennan implied implicitly in the way of responding to Soviet actions, but shared the same vision regarding Stalin's performance.

Charles Bohlen, another American diplomat, and the recipient of the Kennan letter mentioned above, believed in the possibility of reaching a post -war agreement based on the general principles of self -determination and cooperation between the great powers.

Al reconocer la necesidad de mantener la cooperación soviética en el diseño de una solución para la propia Alemania, Bohlen y otros como el secretario de Estado durante la postguerra, James Byrnes, depositaban su fe en la ocupación militar aliada de los antiguos países del Eje y sus satélites, así como en unas elecciones libres conforme a las líneas esbozadas en el Tratado de Yalta.

By recognizing the need to maintain Soviet cooperation in the design of a solution for Germany itself, Bohlen and others such as the Secretary of State during the postwar period, James Byrnes, deposited their faith in the allied military occupation of the ancient countries of the axis and their satellites, as well as in free elections according to the lines outlined in the Yalta treaty.

Solo más adelante, tras observar el funcionamiento del poder soviético bajo los auspicios de los Consejos de Control de los aliados, especialmente en Rumanía y Bulgaria, llegarían a admitir la incompatibilidad de estos objetivos y a compartir la preferencia de Kennan por una Realpolitik de esferas separadas.

Only later, after observing the functioning of the Soviet power under the auspices of the control councils of the allies, especially in Romania and Bulgaria, they would admit the incompatibility of these objectives and to share Kennan's preference for a realpolitik of separate spheres.

Una de las causas que dio origen al optimismo inicial fue la opinión ampliamente extendida de que Stalin no estaba interesado en provocar la confrontación y la guerra.

One of the causes that gave rise to initial optimism was the widely extended opinion that Stalin was not interested in causing confrontation and war.

This was stated by General Eisenhower to President Truman and his Board of Chiefs of Staff in June 1946: «I do not think the reds want war.

En cierto sentido, Eisenhower estaba en lo cierto: Stalin no deseaba entrar en guerra con Estados Unidos (aunque de ello no debía tampoco extraerse la conclusión lógica de que la Unión Soviética tuviera interés en cooperar plenamente con su anterior aliado).

In a sense, Eisenhower was right: Stalin did not want to go to war with the United States (although the logical conclusion should not be extracted that the Soviet Union had interest in fully cooperating with his previous ally).

Y, en todo caso, Estados Unidos, con su monopolio sobre las armas atómicas, no arriesgaba mucho por el hecho de mantener las comunicaciones abiertas con la Unión Soviética y procurar encontrar soluciones compatibles para ambos países a los problemas que compartían.

And, in any case, the United States, with its monopoly on atomic weapons, did not risk much due to maintaining open communications with the Soviet Union and try to find compatible solutions for both countries to the problems they shared.

Otro elemento de la política de Estados Unidos durante la fase inicial de la postguerra lo constituyeron las nuevas instituciones internacionales que los estadounidenses habían contribuido a crear y cuyo éxito deseaban sinceramente.

Another element of the United States policy during the initial post -war phase was the new international institutions that Americans had contributed to create and whose success sincerely wanted.

Las Naciones Unidas, cuyos estatutos se ratificaron el 25 de octubre de 1945 y cuya Asamblea General se reunió por primera vez en enero de 1946, es evidentemente la más conocida; pero fueron los organismos financieros y económicos relacionados con «Bretton Woods» los que quizás revistieron mayor importancia para los responsables políticos de entonces.

The United Nations, whose statutes were ratified on October 25, 1945 and whose general assembly met for the first time in January 1946, is obviously the best known; But it was the financial and economic organizations related to "Bretton Woods" that perhaps more important for the political leaders of that time.

A los ojos de los norteamericanos, el desastre económico de los años de entreguerras parecía constituir la causa raíz de la crisis europea (y mundial).

In the eyes of the Americans, the economic disaster of the years of interwar seemed to constitute the root cause of the European (and world) crisis.

A menos que las divisas fueran convertibles y los diversos países estuvieran dispuestos a beneficiarse mutuamente del incremento del comercio, no había nada que pudiera evitar la vuelta a los terribles días de septiembre de 1931, cuando el sistema monetario posterior a la Primera Guerra Mundial se desmoronó por completo.

Unless the currencies were convertible and the various countries were willing to benefit each other from the increase in trade, there was nothing that could avoid the return to the terrible days of September 1931, when the monetary system after the First World War fell apart completely.

Liderados por Maynard Keynes, el inspirador de la reunión celebrada en el centro de conferencias de Bretton Woods, en New Hampshire, economistas y estadistas trataron de buscar una alternativa al sistema financiero internacional de la época anterior a la guerra: algo menos rígido y deflacionario que el patrón oro, pero más fiable y recíprocamente sostenible que un sistema fluctuante de divisas.

Led by Maynard Keynes, the inspiring of the meeting held at the Bretton Woods Conference Center, in New Hampshire, economists and statesmen tried to find an alternative to the international financial system of the time before the war: somewhat less rigid and deflation than the gold pattern, but more reliable and reciprocally sustainable than a fluctuating system of currencies.

Según Keynes, cualquiera que fuera la forma que adoptara este nuevo sistema, tendría que contar con algo parecido a un banco internacional, que funcionaría de forma similar a un banco central en una economía doméstica, para dirigirlo: es decir, para mantener un tipo de cambio fijo, fomentando y facilitando al mismo tiempo las operaciones de cambio de divisas.

According to Keynes, whatever the way this new system would adopt, it would have to have something similar to an international bank, which would work similar to a central bank in a domestic economy, to direct it: that is, to maintain a fixed exchange rate, encouraging and facilitating at the same time the exchange of currency exchange operations.

Esto es, en esencia, lo que se acordó en Bretton Woods.

That is, in essence, what was agreed in Bretton Woods.

Se creó un Fondo Monetario Internacional (con dinero efectivo estadounidense) «para facilitar la expansión y el crecimiento equilibrado del comercio internacional» (Artículo I).

An international monetary fund (with effective American money) was created "to facilitate the expansion and balanced growth of international trade" (article I).

Su primera junta de gobierno, que tomó como modelo el Consejo de Seguridad de las Naciones Unidas, estuvo integrada por representantes de Estados Unidos, Reino Unido, Francia, China y la URSS.

Its first Government Board, which took as a model the United Nations Security Council, was made up of representatives of the United States, the United Kingdom, France, China and the USSR.

También se propuso una Organización Internacional de Comercio que finalmente se materializaría en 1947 en el Acuerdo General sobre Aranceles y Comercio (transformado más adelante en la Organización Mundial del Comercio).

An international trade organization was also proposed that would finally materialize in 1947 in the General Agreement on Tariffs and Commerce (later transformed into the World Trade Organization).

Sus miembros acordaron los aranceles y otras concesiones aplicables a las partes contratantes, así como unos códigos de prácticas comerciales y procedimientos para tratar posibles incumplimientos y disputas.

Its members agreed to tariffs and other concessions applicable to the contracting parties, as well as some codes of commercial practices and procedures to deal with possible breaches and disputes.

Todo esto suponía en sí mismo una ruptura drástica respecto a los anteriores enfoques «mercantilistas» del comercio, y tenía como fin dar paso, en su día, a una nueva era de libre comercio.

All this supposed in itself a drastic rupture with respect to the previous "mercantilist" approaches to commerce, and was intended to give way, in its day, to a new era of free trade.

Los objetivos e instituciones de Bretton Woods, entre los que también se incluía un nuevo «Banco Mundial», llevaban implícito un grado de injerencia externa en las prácticas nacionales sin precedentes hasta entonces.

Bretton Woods' objectives and institutions, including a new "World Bank", implied an external interference in unprecedented national practices until then.

Por otra parte, las divisas tendrían que ser convertibles en función de su relación con el dólar estadounidense, como condición necesaria para un comercio internacional sostenido y predecible.

On the other hand, currencies would have to be convertible according to their relationship with the US dollar, as a necessary condition for a sustained and predictable international trade.

En la práctica, este punto resultaba problemático: tanto Francia como Gran Bretaña se resistían a la convertibilidad, en el caso británico debido a la existencia de un «área protegida» para su libra esterlina[3] y, en el francés, debido a su sempiterna obsesión por un «franco fuerte» y su deseo de preservar múltiples tipos de cambio para diferentes sectores y productos, la herencia neocolbertiana de una era ya pasada.

In practice, this point was problematic: both France and Great Britain resisted convertibility, in the British case due to the existence of a "protected area" for its pound sterling [3] and, in the French, due to its everlasting obsession with a "strong frank" and its desire to preserve multiple exchange rates for different sectors and products, the neo -Thebert heritage of a past era.

The full convertibility took a decade to be achieved and the Franco and the Libra finally joined the Bretton Woods system in 1958 and 1959 respectively (followed by the German framework, in May 1959, and the Italian lyre in January 1960).

Los participantes de Bretton Woods habían previsto la convertibilidad internacional para finales de la década de 1940, pero sus cálculos no contemplaban las consecuencias políticas y económicas de la llegada de la Guerra Fría (ni, de hecho, del Plan Marshall).

Bretton Woods participants had planned international convertibility by the end of the 1940s, but their calculations did not contemplate the political and economic consequences of the arrival of the Cold War (nor, in fact, of the Marshall Plan).

En otras palabras, los altos ideales de los que establecieron unos planes e instituciones para un mejor sistema internacional daban por hecho una era estable de cooperación internacional de la que todos saldrían beneficiados.

Therefore, when at the beginning of 1946 the Soviet Union abruptly announced that it would not join the institutions of Bretton Woods, the United States Department of the Treasury was truly bewildered; And that was when, to explain the underlying thought to the step that Stalin had taken, George Kennan sent from Moscow, the night of February 22, 1946, his famous long telegram, the first significant testimony of recognition by the United States of the confrontation that was coming.
