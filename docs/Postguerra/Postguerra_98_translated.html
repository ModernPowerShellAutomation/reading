The guarantee of the supplies, the artificially high demand of everything they could manufacture, and the political pressure to function in economically inefficient ways, all led British companies to ruin.

The oil crisis of the early 1970s, the entry into the EEC and the end of the last protected markets of the United Kingdom in their domains and colonies finally ended with the British independent car industry.

The pound - still a very important monetary unit in the international trade of the 1950s - was overvalued, which made Great Britain sell enough abroad to compensate for the chronic deficit of the sterling pound against the dollar.

Gran Bretaña, un país cuya insularidad le hacía sumamente dependiente de las importaciones de alimentos y materias primas básicas, había compensado tradicionalmente su vulnerabilidad estructural con su acceso privilegiado a los mercados protegidos de su imperio y la Commonwealth.

En Europa occidental, la relación del ciudadano con el Estado a lo largo del siglo anterior había constituido un compromiso cambiante entre las necesidades militares y las reivindicaciones políticas: los derechos modernos de unos ciudadanos con un recién adquirido derecho al voto, contrarrestados con las antiguas obligaciones de defender el reino.

In Western Europe, the citizen's relationship with the State throughout the previous century had constituted a changing commitment between military needs and political claims: the modern rights of citizens with a newly acquired right to vote, counteracted with the ancient obligations to defend the kingdom.

Pero a partir de 1945 esa relación se caracterizó cada vez más por un denso tejido de prestaciones sociales y estrategias económicas por el cual era el Estado el que estaba al servicio de los ciudadanos, y no al contrario.

But since 1945 that relationship was increasingly characterized by a dense fabric of social benefits and economic strategies for which it was the State that was at the service of citizens, and not on the contrary.

En años posteriores, las amplias ambiciones de los Estados del bienestar europeos irían perdiendo parte de su atractivo, entre otras cosas porque no podían ya cumplir sus promesas: el desempleo, la inflación, el envejecimiento de la población y la ralentización de la economía impusieron unos límites insoslayables a los esfuerzos de los Estados por cumplir con su parte del trato.

In subsequent years, the broad ambitions of European welfare states would lose part of their attraction, among other things because they could no longer fulfill their promises: unemployment, inflation, population aging and the slowdown of the economy imposed unavoidable limits on the efforts of the states to fulfill their part of the treatment.

The transformations of international capital markets and electronic communications frustrated governments' ability to plan and apply domestic economic policies.

Y, lo que es más importante, la propia legitimidad del Estado intervencionista se vio menoscabada: a escala nacional por los rigores y las ineficiencias de los organismos y las empresas del sector público, y, en el extranjero, por la incontrovertible evidencia del mal funcionamiento económico y la represión política de los Estados socialistas del bloque soviético.

And, more importantly, the legitimacy of the interventionist State was under impaired: at the national level by the rigors and inefficiencies of the organizations and companies of the public sector, and, abroad, due to the incontrovertible evidence of the economic malfunction and the political repression of the socialist states of the Soviet block.
