As for the brilliant generation of economists, philosophers and social thinkers who had flourished in Central Europe before 1934, most of their survivors had permanently exiled to the United States, Great Britain or the antipodes, where they constituted the intellectual core of the "Anglo -Saxon" erudition in their respective fields.

En gran medida estaba relacionada con «interrogarse» (un término artístico contemporáneo) sobre el método y los objetivos de las disciplinas académicas, especialmente de las ciencias sociales —historia, sociología, antropología— pero también de las humanidades, e incluso, años más tarde, de las ciencias experimentales.

In an era of enormous expansion of universities, with publications, magazines and speakers of "monitoring", a market of "theories" of all kinds emerged - stimulated not by an improvement of intellectual supply, but rather by an insatiable demand for consumption.

In the avant -garde of the theoretical revolution were the academic disciplines of history and the most indulgent social sciences.

The renewal of the historical study in Europe had begun a generation before: the Economic History Review and Annales: Économies, Sociétés, Civilizations, whose revisionist project was already implicit in the title, were founded in 1929.

In the 1950s, the group of historians of the Communist Party of Great Britain appeared, the influential social history magazine entitled Past & Present, the Department of Cultural Studies of the University of Birmingham, in England, and the School of Social History created around Hans-Ulrich Wehler at the University of Bielefeld, in West Germany.

El saber generado por los hombres y mujeres asociados con estos grupos e instituciones no era necesariamente iconoclasta; de hecho, a pesar de su muy alta calidad en general, metodológicamente era a menudo bastante convencional.

The knowledge generated by men and women associated with these groups and institutions was not necessarily iconoclast; In fact, despite its very high quality in general, methodologically it was often quite conventional.

It was a history determined by social theory and by the insistence on the importance of social class, especially the lowest classes.

La cuestión no era solo narrar o incluso explicar un momento histórico dado, sino revelar su significado más profundo.

The issue was not only to narrate or even explain a given historical moment, but to reveal its deepest meaning.

Esta línea de estudio histórico parecía acortar la distancia entre el pasado y el presente, entre la especulación intelectual y el compromiso contemporáneo, y una nueva generación de estudiantes adoptó (y, con bastante frecuencia, malinterpretó) esta perspectiva.

This line of historical study seemed to shorten the distance between the past and the present, between intellectual speculation and contemporary commitment, and a new generation of students adopted (and, quite frequently, this perspective misunderstood).

But, despite its political application, history is a discipline especially impervious to high theoretical speculation: the more the theory intervenes, the more the story is retracted.

Inspirándose en una línea anterior de especulación en el terreno de la lingüística, los antropólogos culturales —encabezados por Lévi-Strauss— propusieron una nueva explicación globalizada de las variaciones y las diferencias entre las distintas sociedades.

Inspired by a previous line of speculation in the field of linguistics, cultural anthropologists-found by Lévi-Strauss-proposed a new globalized explanation of variations and differences between different societies.

Lo que importaba no eran los usos sociales o los síntomas culturales, sino la esencia interna, la estructura profunda de los asuntos humanos.

What mattered were not social uses or cultural symptoms, but the internal essence, the deep structure of human affairs.

"Structuralism," as it was called, exercised a powerful seduction.

Como los admiradores de Lévi-Strauss de otras disciplinas relacionadas explicaban, el estructuralismo no era siquiera una teoría representacional: los códigos o «signos» sociales que describía no se relacionaban con ninguna persona, lugar o hecho concreto, sino sencillamente con otros signos, con los que formaba un sistema cerrado.

As Lévi-Strauss admirers of other related disciplines explained, structuralism was not even a representational theory: the social codes or "signs" that it described did not relate to any person, concrete place or fact, but simply to other signs, with which it formed a closed system.

Por tanto, no estaba sujeto a la comprobación o desaprobación empírica —en el estructuralismo no podía demostrarse nunca que algo estuviera equivocado— y la iconoclasta ambición de sus afirmaciones, unidas a su impermeabilidad a la contradicción, garantizaban un público numeroso.

Therefore, it was not subject to empirical verification or disapproval - in structuralism, it could never be demonstrated that something was wrong - and the iconoclastic ambition of their statements, together with its impermeability to the contradiction, guaranteed a large audience.

His best -known representatives - generally those who knew how to combine in the appropriate doses the boldness of their erudition with a natural talent for self -promotion - became international celebrities, with the good fortune that their entry into the intellectual candlestick coincided with the beginning of the television phenomenon as a mass medium.
