In popular democracies, the possestalinist era was marked not only by the 1953 Berlin revolt (see Chapter VI) but by the opposition that arose even in redoubts of the empire as separated and usually as docile as the provincial Bulgaria, where tobacco factories workers were mutinied in May and June of that same year.

Although none of these revolts was a serious threat to the Soviet Union, the Moscow authorities took the degree of public discontent.

The task that Jrushchov and his colleagues were now faced was to bury Stalin and his excesses without putting the system that the Stalinist terror had built and the advantages that the party had reported his monopoly of power.

Jrushchov's strategy, as would be evidenced in later years, covered four objectives.

First, as we have already seen, it was necessary to stabilize relations with the West after the rearmament of West Germany, its incorporation into NATO and the signing of the Warsaw Pact.

At the same time, Moscow began to build bridges with the "non-aligned" world-complying with Yugoslavia, to which Jruscov and Marshal Bulganin made a visit in May 1955 (only one month after signing the treaty of the Austrian state) in order to resume Soviet-Yugoslav relations after three years of cold stagnation.

En tercer lugar, Moscú comenzó a animar a los reformistas del Partido en los Estados satélite, permitiendo críticas moderadas a los «errores» de la vieja guardia estalinista y la rehabilitación de algunas de sus víctimas, y con el fin del ciclo de juicios-espectáculo, arrestos masivos y purgas dentro del Partido.

Thirdly, Moscow began to encourage the reformists of the party in the satellite states, allowing moderate criticism for the "errors" of the old Stalinist guard and the rehabilitation of some of their victims, and with the end of the cycle of judgments-spectacle, mass arrests and purges within the party.

Así estaban las cosas cuando Jruschov procedió cautelosamente a la cuarta (y a su modo de entender, definitiva) fase de reformas controladas: la ruptura con el propio Stalin.

Thus were things when Jrushchov proceeded cautiously to the fourth (and in his way of understanding, definitive) phase of controlled reforms: the break with Stalin himself.

El escenario escogido para ello fue el vigésimo Congreso del Partido Comunista de la Unión Soviética, celebrado en febrero de 1956, en el que Jruschov pronunció su hoy famoso «discurso secreto», en el que denunciaba los crímenes y los errores del secretario general y el «culto» a su persona.

The scenario chosen for this was the twenty -congress of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, held in February 1956, in which Jrushchov pronounced his famous "secret speech", in which he denounced the crimes and mistakes of the Secretary General and the "cult" of his person.

Visto desde la retrospectiva, este discurso ha adquirido un aura mítica, pero su trascendencia no debería exagerarse.

Seen from retrospective, this speech has acquired a mythical aura, but its transcendence should not exaggerate.

Nikita Jruschov era un comunista, un leninista y su fe no era menos firme que la del resto de sus coetáneos de la jefatura del Partido.

Nikita Jruschov was a communist, a Leninist and her faith was no less firm than the rest of her contemporaries of the party's leadership.

The delicate objective that had been proposed was to recognize and detail the acts of Stalin, and make all the responsibility of said acts fall on his person.

His task, in his view, was to reaffirm the legitimacy of the communist project pouring all the opprobrium and guilt about the body of the "uncle Pepe."

The speech, pronounced on February 25, was absolutely conventional in terms of extension and language.

The dictator was accused of "ignoring the rules of operation of the party and trampling the leninist principles of the collective direction of the party": that is, of making their own decisions.

Sus colegas más jóvenes (entre los cuales se había encontrado Jruschov desde comienzos de la década de 1930), quedaban de este modo absueltos de responsabilidad tanto en lo referente a sus excesos criminales como, y más importante, al fracaso de sus políticas.

His younger colleagues (among which Jrushchov had been found since the beginning of the 1930s), thus were acquitted of responsibility both in relation to their criminal excesses and, and more importantly, to the failure of their policies.

Jruschov asumió el riesgo calculado de detallar el alcance de los fracasos personales de Stalin (lo que provocó la consiguiente conmoción y ofensa en la sensibilidad de los obedientes dirigentes que se encontraban entre el público), a fin de preservar e incluso realzar la inmaculada imagen de Lenin, el sistema de gobierno leninista y los propios sucesores de Stalin.

Jrushchov assumed the calculated risk of detailing the scope of Stalin's personal failures (which caused the consequent shock and offense in the sensitivity of the obedient leaders who were among the public), in order to preserve and even enhance the immaculate image of Lenin, the Leninist government system and the Successors of Stalin themselves.

El discurso secreto consiguió su propósito, al menos dentro del Partido Comunista de la Unión Soviética, y trazó una clara línea de ruptura respecto a la era estalinista, que reconocía sus monstruosidades y desastres al tiempo que mantenía la ficción de que los actuales líderes comunistas estaban exentos de toda responsabilidad.

The secret speech achieved its purpose, at least within the Communist Party of the Soviet Union, and drew a clear line of rupture with respect to the Stalinist era, which recognized its monstrosities and disasters while maintaining the fiction that the current communist leaders were exempt from all responsibility.

De este modo, Jruschov se mantenía a salvo en el poder y conseguía en cierta medida carta blanca para reformar la economía soviética y liberalizar el aparato del terror.

In this way, Jrushchov remained safe in power and achieved to some extent Carta Blanca to reform the Soviet economy and liberalize the terror apparatus.
