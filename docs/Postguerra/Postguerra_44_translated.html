According to Schuman's plan, high authority would have the power to promote competition and determine the price policy, direct investment and purchase and sale in the name of the participating countries.

Quai d’Orsay found in it a sweet satisfaction: it was the first of a long series of small reprisals against the Anglo -American decisions that had been taken without consulting Paris.

Los tres jefes de Estado de los tres Estados miembros más importantes, Alcide de Gasperi, Konrad Adenauer y Robert Schuman procedían de las regiones limítrofes de sus países: De Gasperi del Trentino, al nordeste de Italia, Adenauer de Renania y Schuman de la Lorena.

For all three, as well as for their democratic democrats of the Luxembourg bilingual, the Belgium Bilingual and Multicultural and Holland, a European cooperation project made sense both from the cultural and economic point of view, since, as was logical, they considered it as a contribution to overcome the crisis of civilization that had shaken the cosmopolitan Europe of its youth.

The common origin of border regions of their respective countries, where identities had been multiple and fungible borders, made Schuman and their colleagues not especially disturb them the perspective of reaching some kind of fusion of national sovereignty.

Kenneth Younger, former Bevin advisor, scored in his newspaper on May 14, 1950 (five days after hearing the Schuman Plan) that although in general he was in favor of Europe's economic integration, the new proposals could constitute "on the other hand, [...] one more step towards the consolidation of the Catholic" black "to which I have always believed a motor force that was behind the Council of Europe."

As Adenauer explained to Macmillan ten years later, the mint was not even actually an economic organization (and Great Britain, in his opinion, had properly done keeping out of it).

A year later, in 1950, this commercial balance was negative, since Germany was consuming its own raw materials to supply the local industry.

In 1951, the balance was positive again, and thus it would remain for many years, thanks to the German export of manufactured products.

For some time, London had clearly expressed himself in this regard: in 1948, when Bevin debated with the Ministerial Cabinet of Labor the US proposals for a future European Economic Cooperation Organization, his main concern was that “effective control was in the hands of national delegations […] to prevent the Secretariat (or an“ independent ”president) to act on his own […] In no case could the organization individual members ».
