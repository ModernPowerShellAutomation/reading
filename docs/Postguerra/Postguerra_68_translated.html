For military and economic reasons, the British governments of the time were very interested in changing from a continental defense strategy to another nuclear deterrence; Indeed, the British insistence had played an important role in persuading Eisenhower to devise their strategy of the "new image", and the British did not put any objection to the installation of American bombers with nuclear capacity on British soil [7].

The French also had an atomic bomb program, approved by Mendès-France in December 1954, although the first French independent bomb was not successfully exploited until February 1960.
