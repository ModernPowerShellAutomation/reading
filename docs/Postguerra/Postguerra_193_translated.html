Privado de su portentoso preámbulo giscardiano (que inmediatamente se comparó, para su mal, con la elegante brevedad de su antecesor jeffersoniano), el documento de la Convención apenas tenía elementos que pudieran considerarse propuestas constitucionales clásicas: no había definiciones amplias de libertad individual ni proclamaciones claras sobre la división de poderes.

Deprived of his portentous Giscardian preamble (which was immediately compared, for his evil, with the elegant brevity of his Jeffersonian predecessor), the convention document barely had elements that could be considered classical constitutional proposals: there were no broad definitions of individual freedom or clear proclamations on the division of powers.

En este sentido, como muchos habían pronosticado, era decepcionante.

In this sense, as many had predicted, it was disappointing.

Pero el texto de Giscard —que después de algunos debates se aprobó en Roma como Tratado Constitucional en 2004— sí servía como plan de trabajo para una gestión práctica de los asuntos de la Unión: mejoraba los sistemas de coordinación en materias como defensa o inmigración y proporcionaba un resumen unitario de las leyes de la Unión Europea, una Carta de Derechos Fundamentales de los Ciudadanos de la Unión Europea, destinada a fortalecer aún más la autoridad de los tribunales europeos, y una clara e incluso ambiciosa explicación de las competencias y de la autoridad formales de la Unión.

But Giscard's text - which after some debates was approved in Rome as a constitutional treaty in 2004 - did serve as a work plan for a practical management of union affairs: it improved coordination systems in matters such as defense or immigration and provided a unit summary of the laws of the European Union, a letter of fundamental rights of the citizens of the European Union, aimed to strengthen the authority of the European court Even ambitious explanation of the formal powers and authority of the Union.

Sobre todo, la propuesta de Constitución habría servido para reducir —con el tiempo— el inestable sistema de representación nacional de la Comisión; e ideaba un sistema de votación para el Consejo Europeo que, después de ciertos regateos, resultó aceptable para todas las partes y también demográficamente equitativo.

Above all, the constitution proposal would have served to reduce - with the time - the unstable system of national representation of the Commission; and devised a voting system for the European Council that, after certain bargaining, was acceptable to all parties and also demographically equitable.

Seguía sin estar claro que las nuevas disposiciones fueran a producir mayorías claras en momentos difíciles: mucho menos cuando, en asuntos polémicos como los impuestos y la defensa, se acordó —ante la insistencia británica, pero con el callado alivio de muchos otros países— conservar el viejo mecanismo gaullista de los vetos nacionales.

It was still unclear that the new provisions were to produce clear majorities in difficult times: much less when, in controversial matters such as taxes and defense, it agreed - before the British insistence, but with the silent relief of many other countries - to preserve the old Gaullist mechanism of national vetoes.

Además, a nadie le cabía duda de que a pesar de la cuidadosa distribución de votos ponderados, el poder real lo seguían ostentando los países principales: según la conclusión a la que Ortega y Gasset había llegado ya en los años treinta, por «Europa se entiende, ante todo y propiamente, la trinidad Francia, Inglaterra, Alemania».

In addition, no one had doubt that despite the careful distribution of weighted votes, the real power continued to hold the main countries: according to the conclusion to which Ortega y Gasset had already arrived in the thirties, by "Europe it is understood, first and foremost, the Trinity France, England, Germany."

Sin embargo, ahora sería posible, por lo menos, tomar decisiones, presuponiendo siempre que la Constitución fuera ratificada en todos los Estados miembros, un impedimento que, como se vería, nadie había previsto.

However, now it would be possible, at least, to make decisions, always presupposing that the Constitution was ratified in all Member States, an impediment that, as would be seen, nobody had planned.

De este modo, en 2004 la Unión Europea, ante la sorpresa de muchos observadores, parecía haber superado o al menos aliviado las dificultades prácticas que comportaba gobernar una comunidad inmanejable e incipiente de veinticinco Estados independientes.

Thus, in 2004 the European Union, before the surprise of many observers, seemed to have overcome or at least relieve the practical difficulties that an unmanageable and incipient community of twenty -five independent states.

Sin embargo, lo que no se había hecho —lo que ni siquiera habían comenzado a hacer ni la Convención de Giscard, ni los diversos tratados, ni la Comisión y sus variopintos informes y programas, ni las costosas publicaciones y páginas web diseñadas para educar al público europeo sobre la Unión y su funcionamiento— era abordar la crónica falta de interés en la Unión Europea de la población europea.

However, what had not been done - which had not even begun to make the Giscard convention, nor the various treaties, nor the commission and its diverse reports and programs, nor the expensive publications and web pages designed to educate the European public about the union and its operation - was to address the chronic lack of interest in the European Union of the European population.

If the technocrats who built the institutions of the new "Europe" had shown a haughty disinterest in the opinions of citizenship as a whole, this feeling was now returned in kind and conscientious.

British Prime Minister Clement Attlee, bleakly reflecting the obsession that his Labor colleagues had for the techniques and norms of the political management of his party, used to prevent against the "fundamental fallacy" of believing that "the elaboration of a machinery allows to escape the need to trust other human beings [10]".

The European Union suffered a serious "democratic deficit."

Every time elections to the European Parliament were celebrated, participation fell and the only exceptions to this standard occurred when those elections coincided with the nationals, at which time the voters who had mobilized for local or national issues took the opportunity to vote also in the European elections.

Why did the Europeans, "old" and "new" be so deeply indifferent towards the affairs of the European Union?
