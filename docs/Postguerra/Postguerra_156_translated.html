El nivel de vida en Checoslovaquia, Hungría y Polonia mejoró a lo largo de los años setenta, al menos en lo tocante al consumo al por menor.

The standard of living in Czechoslovakia, Hungary and Poland improved throughout the seventies, at least in terms of retail consumption.

El número de coches y de televisiones —los bienes de consumo duraderos más simbólicos de la época— aumentaba constantemente: en Polonia, el número de coches privados per cápita se multiplicó por cuatro entre 1975 y 1989.

The number of cars and televisions - the most symbolic consumer consumer goods of the time - constantly increased: in Poland, the number of private cars per capita multiplied by four between 1975 and 1989.

A finales de los ochenta había cuatro televisiones por cada diez personas en Hungría, y las cifras en Checoslovaquia eran parecidas.

At the end of the eighties there were four televisions for every ten people in Hungary, and the figures in Czechoslovakia were similar.

En general, si los compradores estaban dispuestos a aceptar una calidad inferior, un diseño insulso y poca variedad, al final podían encontrar lo que deseaban, en tiendas oficiales o a través del sector «privado».

In general, if buyers were willing to accept a lower quality, a bland design and little variety, in the end they could find what they wanted, in official stores or through the "private" sector.

Sin embargo, en la Unión Soviética, esos bienes «opcionales» eran más difíciles de encontrar, y relativamente más caros.

However, in the Soviet Union, those "optional" goods were more difficult to find, and relatively more expensive.

Lo mismo podía decirse de las necesidades básicas.

The same could be said of basic needs.

En marzo de 1979 un comprador de Washington DC tenía que trabajar 12,5 horas para poder sufragar la típica cesta de la compra de alimentos básicos (con salchichas, leche, huevos, patatas, verdura, té, cerveza, etcétera).

In March 1979, a Washington DC buyer had to work 12.5 hours to cover the typical basket basket of basic foods (with sausages, milk, eggs, potatoes, vegetables, tea, beer, etc.).

Una cesta similar costaba 21,4 horas en Londres, pero 42,3 en Moscú, a pesar de la cuantía de las subvenciones[15].

A similar basket cost 21.4 hours in London, but 42.3 in Moscow, despite the amount of subsidies [15].

Además, el consumidor soviético o el de Europa del Este tenía que pasarse muchas más horas buscando y comprando su comida y otros artículos.

In addition, the Soviet consumer or that of Eastern Europe had to spend many more hours looking for and buying their food and other items.

La vida en el comunismo, si se medía en tiempo y esfuerzo, aunque no en rublos, coronas o florines húngaros, era tan cara como agotadora.

Life in communism, if measured in time and effort, although not in rubles, crowns or Hungarian florins, was as expensive as exhausting.

El problema de definir el comunismo por su éxito a la hora de satisfacer a los consumidores privados radicaba en que toda la economía, como ya hemos observado, se orientaba a producir maquinaría industrial y materias primas.

The problem of defining communism for its success when satisfying private consumers was that the entire economy, as we have already observed, was aimed at producing industrial machinery and raw materials.

Las economías comunistas, a excepción de los alimentos, no producían cosas que los consumidores quisieran (y tampoco eran muy eficientes produciendo comida: hacía tiempo que la Unión Soviética se había convertido en importadora neta de cereales, triplicando, solo entre 1970 y 1982, sus importaciones de productos alimentarios).

The communist economies, with the exception of food, did not produce things that consumers would like (and were not very efficient producing food: the Soviet Union had long become a net importer of cereals, tripling, only between 1970 and 1982, its imports of food products).

La única forma de esquivar este obstáculo era importar bienes de consumo del exterior, pero había que pagarlos en divisas fuertes, que solo podían adquirirse exportando, y el mercado mundial, a excepción del petróleo soviético, apenas demandaba productos socialistas, a menos que se vendieran a precios muy reducidos, y, en la mayoría de los casos, ni siquiera así.

The only way to dodge this obstacle was to import consumer goods from abroad, but they had to pay them in strong currencies, which could only be acquired exporting, and the world market, with the exception of Soviet oil, barely demanded socialist products, unless they were sold at very small prices, and, in most cases, not even so.

En la práctica, la única forma de llenar los expositores del Este era pedir dinero a Occidente.

In practice, the only way to fill the exhibitors of the East was to ask the West.

No hay duda de que Occidente estaba muy dispuesto a cumplir.

There is no doubt that the West was very willing to comply.

Tanto el FMI como el Banco Mundial y los bancos privados estaban encantados de hacer préstamos al bloque soviético: el Ejército Rojo era una tranquilizadora garantía de estabilidad y los cargos comunistas falseaban la producción y los recursos de sus países hasta resultar convincentes[16].

Both the IMF and the World Bank and private banks were delighted to make loans to the Soviet bloc: the Red Army was a reassuring stability guarantee and communist positions falsified the production and resources of their countries until they were convincing [16].

Solo a lo largo de la década de 1970 la deuda en divisas fuertes de Checoslovaquia se multiplicó por doce.

The Poland increased by 3000 percent, while the first secretary Gierek and his colleagues absorbed subsidized Western products, applied expensive social programs for the peasants and frozen food prices at the levels of 1965.

Una vez desatado este grado de endeudamiento, era difícil contenerlo.

Once this degree of indebtedness was unleashed, it was difficult to contain it.

Los incrementos de los precios de los alimentos aprobados por Gierek en 1976 provocaron furiosos disturbios y fueron rápidamente revocados, con lo que el régimen optó por seguir pidiendo préstamos: entre 1977 y 1980 un tercio de la línea crediticia externa de Polonia se utilizó para subvencionar el consumo interno.

The increases in food prices approved by Gierek in 1976 caused furious disturbances and were quickly revoked, so the regime chose to continue asking for loans: between 1977 and 1980 a third of the external credit line of Poland was used to subsidize internal consumption.

Los economistas comunistas de Praga recomendaron la supresión de las subvenciones y la introducción de precios reales, pero sus jefes políticos tuvieron miedo de las consecuencias sociales de esa medida y prefirieron incrementar los préstamos.

The communist economists of Prague recommended the suppression of subsidies and the introduction of real prices, but their political bosses were afraid of the social consequences of that measure and preferred to increase loans.

Al igual que en el periodo de entreguerras, los frágiles y pequeños estados de Europa oriental solicitaban una vez más créditos a Occidente para financiar sus economías autárquicas y evitar decisiones difíciles.

As in the interwar period, the fragile and small states of Eastern Europe requested once again credits to the West to finance their autarchic economies and avoid difficult decisions.

Así lo reconocería años después Miklós Németh, el último primer ministro comunista de Hungría.

This would recognize it years later Miklós Németh, the last communist prime minister of Hungary.

A loan of one billion German frameworks granted by BONN in October 1987, that Western Germans disguised as a contribution to the Hungarian economic "reform", was really used as follows: "We spent two thirds to pay interest and the rest to import consumer goods to relieve the printing of economic crisis."

In 1986 the Hungarian official deficit in current account was already one thousand four hundred million dollars annually.

Entre 1971 y 1980 la deuda en divisas fuertes de Polonia había pasado de mil millones de dólares a veinte mil quinientos millones, y lo peor estaba por llegar.

Between 1971 and 1980 the debt in strong currencies of Poland had gone from one billion dollars to twenty thousand five hundred million, and the worst was yet to come.

Según la propia República Democrática reconoció, en sus últimos años gastaba el 60 por ciento de sus ganancias anuales por exportación en abonar los intereses (muy generosamente reducidos) de sus deudores occidentales.

According to the democratic republic herself recognized, in her last years she spent 60 percent of her annual export profits on paying interest (very generously reduced) of her western debtors.

Yugoslavia, un cliente siempre preferencial (entre 1950 y 1964 Estados Unidos había cubierto tres quintos del déficit anual de Belgrado), recibió préstamos generosos y créditos de emergencia amparados en datos oficiales que no se parecían ni por asomo a la realidad.

Yugoslavia, an always preferential client (between 1950 and 1964 the United States had covered three fifths of the annual Belgrade deficit), received generous loans and emergency credits protected by official data that did not even resembled reality.

En conjunto, la deuda en divisas fuertes de Europa oriental, que representaba seis mil cien millones de dólares en 1971, pasó a sesenta y seis mil en 1980, y en 1988 llegaría a noventa y cinco mil seiscientos millones.

En estas cifras, que podrían haber sido aún peores de no ser por cierta flexibilidad en los precios introducida en Hungría durante los setenta, no aparecía Rumanía, donde Ceauşescu había cargado la cancelación de la deuda exterior de su país sobre las espaldas de sus siempre sufrientes súbditos.

In these figures, which could have been worse because it was not for some flexibility in the prices introduced in Hungary during the seventies, Romania did not appear, where Ceauşescu had loaded the cancellation of his country's foreign debt on the back of his always suffering subjects.

But the message was clear: the communist system not only lived on loans, but of a time I no longer had.

Tarde o temprano sería necesario aplicar ajustes económicos dolorosos y socialmente perturbadores.

Sooner or later it would be necessary to apply painful and socially disturbing economic adjustments.

En los años venideros, el famoso jefe de espías germano-oriental Markus Wolf afirmó que a finales de los años setenta él ya había llegado a la conclusión de que la República Democrática «no funcionaba» y no hay duda de que no era el único.

In the coming years, the famous germano-organic spies Markus Wolf said that in the late 1970s he had already concluded that the democratic republic "did not work" and there is no doubt that he was not the only one.

Economists such as Hungarian Tamas Bauer and his Contemporary Polish Leszek Balcelerowicz knew perfectly how fragile the castle of communist cards was already.

But as long as the capitalists finance it, communism could survive.

The "stagnation time" (in the words of Gorbachev) by Leónidas Brézhnev fed many illusions, and not only in his country.

In a new era of realism, there could be no more realistic characters than Edward Gierek in Warsaw (born in 1913), Gustav Husák in Prague (born in 1913), Erich Honecker in Berlin (1912), János Kádá (1908) and Josip Broz Tito in Belgrade (1892).

Like Leónidas Brézhnev - born in 1906, awarded seven times with the order of Lenin and in four with the Hero Medal of the Soviet Union, winner of the Lenin de la Paz Prize, general secretary and, since 1977, head of the State - those men had been raised with the old customs.

His choice aroused unpublished expectations in the contemporary era.
