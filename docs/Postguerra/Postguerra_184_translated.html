This generational lag would have political consequences in all countries, with major and poor voters who periodically were prone to be attracted to parties that offered nostalgic or ultra -nationalist alternatives to the new liberal consensus.

It was foreseeable that this problem was more serious in certain areas of the former Soviet Union, where disorders and disturbances were more pronounced and where, until that moment, democracy was something unknown.

Miserables, inseguros y dolidos por la llamativa riqueza que ahora mostraba una minúscula minoría, los votantes ancianos —y los que no lo eran tanto—, en especial de Rusia y Ucrania, eran fácilmente atraídos por políticos autoritarios.

Miserable, insecure and hurt by the striking wealth that now showed a tiny minority, the elderly voters - and those who were not so much - especially from Russia and Ukraine, were easily attracted to authoritarian politicians.

De manera que, mientras en las tierras poscomunistas resultaba bastante fácil inventarse constituciones y partidos democráticos modélicos, otra cosa muy distinta era forjar un electorado que discriminara.

So, while in the postcommunist lands it was quite easy to invent constitutions and modelic democratic parties, another very different thing was to forge an electorate to discriminate.

En líneas generales y en todas partes, las primeras elecciones favorecieron a las alianzas liberales o de centro derecha que habían gestionado el derrocamiento del antiguo régimen; pero, con frecuencia, la violenta reacción ocasionada por las penurias económicas y las inevitables decepciones benefició a los excomunistas, ahora vestidos con atuendos nacionalistas.

In general and everywhere, the first elections favored liberal or center -right alliances that had managed the overthrow of the old regime; But, often, the violent reaction caused by economic hardships and the inevitable disappointments benefited the excommunists, now dressed in nationalist outfits.

This transformation of the old nomenklature was less peculiar than it might seem abroad.

El nacionalismo y el comunismo tenían más rasgos comunes entre sí que los que cada uno de ellos tenía con la democracia: compartían, por así decirlo, una «sintaxis» política, mientras que el liberalismo era un lenguaje completamente distinto.

Nationalism and communism had more common features that each of them had with democracy: they shared, so to speak, a political "syntax", while liberalism was a completely different language.

Como mínimo, el comunismo soviético y los nacionalistas tradicionales tenían un enemigo común —el capitalismo u «Occidente»— y sus herederos supieron manipular con destreza un igualitarismo generalizado y envidioso («por lo menos entonces todos éramos pobres») para atribuir los males del poscomunismo a la injerencia extranjera.

At least, Soviet communism and traditional nationalists had a common enemy - capitalism or "west" - and their heirs knew how to manipulate a generalized and envious egalitarianism ("at least then then we were all poor") to attribute the evils of postcommunism to foreign interference.

Los políticos nacionalistas, incluso los que habían partido de las críticas al comunismo —como fue el caso del movimiento nacional patriótico ruso Pamiat (Memoria)—, caían con bastante facilidad en una simbiótica simpatía hacia el pasado soviético, que conjugaba una especie de resentimiento nacionalista con la nostalgia del legado soviético y de sus monumentos.

Nationalist politicians, even those who had left criticism of communism - as was the case of the Russian patriotic national movement Pamiat (memory) -, fell quite easily into a symbiotic symbiotic sympathy towards the Soviet past, which combined a kind of nationalist resentment with the nostalgia of the Soviet legacy and its monuments.

That same combination of patriotic rhetoric and sadness for having lost the authoritarian world of Soviet wedge explained the popularity of the new Ukrainian nationalists, Belarus, Serbs and Slovaks, as well as that of their equivalents in the various agrarian and popular parties that proliferated in Poland in the late nineties, especially the Self -Defense Party of Andrzej Lepper had.

He was not surprising: in the words of Vladimir Zhirinovsky, a new and burning public character who cemented his electoral support in a blatant xenophobia in the old Russian style, "the Russian people have become the most humiliated nation on the planet."

Cualesquiera que hubieran sido sus limitaciones, la Unión Soviética había sido una potencia mundial: un gigante territorial y cultural, heredero legítimo y prolongación de la Rusia imperial.

Whatever its limitations, the Soviet Union had been a world power: a territorial and cultural giant, legitimate heir and prolongation of imperial Russia.

His disintegration caused great affronts to the old Russians, who in many cases shared the resentment felt by the Soviet army both for the absorption made by the NATO of the Russian "west" and for the inability of their country to prevent it.

The desire to recover some kind of international "respect" has oriented much of Moscow's foreign policy after communism and explains both the nature of the presidency of Vladimir Putin and the broad support with which he could count, despite the growing authoritarianism of his internal policy (and also because of him).

In other countries, even the media of the former communist era were seen with some love: in 2004, the most audience program of Czech television was a replacement of Major Zeman, a police series from the beginning of the seventies whose scripts were little more than propaganda exercises for the period of "normalization" after 1968.

The Communist Party only had the impudence of conserving its name in the Czech Republic (in addition to France and the states of the former Soviet Union).

In Balkans, specifically, "anti -Americanism" or "antieuropeism" used to be a nickname of anti -capitalism, a cover for excommunists who could not openly express nostalgia for the old days, but that exploited it anyway in their masked public pronouncements.

Few differences were regarding these objectives among the main contending parties, which always won the elections criticizing the "failed" policies of their adversaries, and then proceeding to implement surprisingly similar programs.

In Central Europe, this caused the emergence of a new "stubborn" language on public policies - "democracy", "market", "budget deficit", "growth" and "competition" -, which, for many citizens, had barely meaning or interest.

A comienzos de los noventa, para los observadores, el ascenso en la Europa poscomunista de partidos nacionalpopulistas marginales y de sus demagógicos líderes representaba una peligrosa reacción antidemocrática, el retorno a sentimientos atávicos de una región atrasada que durante medio siglo había estado presa de la ilusión de que el tiempo se había detenido.

At the beginning of the nineties, for observers, the promotion in the post -communist Europe of national marginal parties and their leading demagogics represented a dangerous antidemocratic reaction, the return to atavistic feelings of a backward region that for half a century had been prey to the illusion that time had stopped.

The success of political democracy in many countries of the ancient communist bloc had ambivalent consequences for the intellectuals who had done so much for their establishment.
