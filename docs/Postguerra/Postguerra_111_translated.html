En lo que cada vez se conocía más con el nombre de «Tercer Mundo» había candidatos alternativos: los nacionalistas anticolonialistas del norte de África y Oriente Próximo; los radicales negros de Estados Unidos (difícilmente catalogables como del Tercer Mundo, pero que se sentían estrechamente identificados con él), y las guerrillas campesinas repartidas por el mundo entero, desde América Central hasta el Mar del Sur de China.

In what was increasingly known with the name of "Third World" there were alternative candidates: the anti -colonial nationalists of North Africa and the Middle East; The black radicals of the United States (hardly classible as the Third World, but who felt closely identified with him), and the peasant guerrillas distributed throughout the world, from Central America to the South China Sea.

Junto con los «estudiantes» e incluso los jóvenes en general, estos constituían un contingente más numeroso y más dispuesto a movilizarse en pro de unas esperanzas revolucionarias que la serias y satisfechas masas trabajadoras del próspero Occidente.

Together with the "students" and even young people in general, they constituted a more numerous contingent and more willing to mobilize for revolutionary hopes than the serious and satisfied working masses of the prosperous West.

A partir de 1956 los jóvenes radicales de Europa occidental se alejaron de la desalentadora experiencia comunista de la Europa del Este para buscar inspiración en lugares más lejanos.

From 1956 the young radicals of Western Europe moved away from the discouraging communist experience of Eastern Europe to seek inspiration in farther places.

Este nuevo gusto por lo exótico se vio impulsado en parte por la descolonización de la época y las aspiraciones de los movimientos nacionales de liberación, y en parte por la proyección sobre otros de las propias ilusiones perdidas de Europa.

This new taste for the exotic was partly driven by the decolonization of the time and the aspirations of the national liberation movements, and partly by the projection on other of the lost illusions of Europe.

El conocimiento de las circunstancias locales era notablemente escaso, pese a la emergencia de una rudimentaria industria académica de «estudios campesinos».

The knowledge of local circumstances was remarkably scarce, despite the emergence of a rudimentary academic industry of "peasant studies."

Las revoluciones de Cuba y China fueron especialmente investidas de todas las cualidades y los logros de los que lamentablemente carecía Europa.

The revolutions of Cuba and China were especially invested with all the qualities and achievements of which unfortunately lacked Europe.

The Italian Marxist writer Maria-Antonietta Macciocchi commented ecstatic the contrast between the miserable situation of contemporary Europe and the post-revolutionary utopia of the China of Mao, then in full a peak of the cultural revolution: «In China there are no symptoms of alienation, nerve disorders or fragmentation of the individual who are in the consumer society.

El mundo de los chinos es compacto, integrado y absolutamente sano».

The world of the Chinese is compact, integrated and absolutely healthy ».

The peasant revolutions in the non -European world also presented another characteristic that attracted the intellectuals and European students of the time: they were violent.

The violence of Third World uprisings was a liberating violence.

As Jean-Paul Sartre explained in the prologue to the French edition of Frantz Fanon, the condemned of the earth, in the violence of the anticolonial revolutions «The man recreated himself […] to shoot a European is to kill two birds of a shot, end at the same time with the oppressor and with the man whom he oppresses: thus leaving a dead man and a free man; The survivor, for the first time, feels under his feet a national soil ».

Esta abnegada admiración por modelos ajenos no era nueva en Europa: Tocqueville había comentado en su momento la atracción que la intelligentsia prerrevolucionaria de la Francia del siglo XVIII había sentido por otros modelos, y un sentimiento similar había desempeñado un papel importante en la seducción que produjo la propia revolución soviética.

This selfless admiration for foreign models was not new in Europe: Tocqueville had commented at the time the attraction that prerevolutionary intelligentsia of the 17th -century France had felt for other models, and a similar feeling had played an important role in the seduction produced by the Soviet revolution itself.

Radical students from Milan and Berlin were urged to imitate the eastern oriental stratagems of success: in a significant combination of Maoist rhetoric and Trotskyist tactics, the German student leader Rudi Dutschke made in 1968 an appeal for his followers to star in "a long march through the institutions."

For its conservative leaders, these occasional invocations of external models illustrated the undisciplined ease with which the venerable revolutionary syntax of old Europe was becoming an ideological Babel.

American defense expenses increased constantly in the mid -1960s, and reached their maximum in 1968.

The Vietnam War did not generate divisions in Europe - all the political spectrum showed its disapproval - but served as a catalyst for the mobilization of the entire continent: even in Britain, where the most numerous manifestations of the decade were explicitly convened to oppose US politics.

In the Solidarity Campaign with Vietnam of 1968, several tens of thousands of students toured the streets of London in the direction of the American embassy of Grosvenor Square to angrily demand the end of the war in Vietnam (and the reluctant support of this of the British Labor Government).

Thanks to the two -laps legislative elections system and the president's election for universal suffrage, French political life had merged into the 1960s in a stable system of electoral and parliamentary coalitions built around two political families: the communists and socialist of the left, and the centrist and gaullists of the right.
