The country was then directed by and for the "new class", as the Yugoslav dissident Milovan Djilas called her in an influential book of 1957: an educated technocracy of bureaucrats and professionals, pragmatically concentrated in sweeping inward and ensuring her own survival.

The authentic liberation was unthinkable, but a return to repression seemed highly unlikely.

The Hungary of Kádár - "the best barracks in the camp" -, despite being the subject of the envy of many, was emulated in a quite irregular way.

The second model, the Yugoslavia de Tito, was even more obviously Sui Generis.

This was not because Yugoslavia would have managed to avoid the problems of her neighbors.

Many of the economic failures of the Soviet satellites were also known to the Yugoslavs, a reminder that the vegetative state between the east and the west in which the country was was the product of historical chance rather than the ideological option.

These innovations were the fruit of both ethnic and geographical divisions and economic need.

En un Estado federal integrado por unas repúblicas y unos ciudadanos que apenas compartían nada aparte de unos recuerdos tristes y antagónicos entre sí, la imposición de unas normas uniformes desde Belgrado parecía en gran medida una vuelta a las prácticas de la preguerra.

In a federal state composed of republics and citizens who barely shared anything apart from sad and antagonistic memories with each other, the imposition of uniform norms from Belgrade seemed largely a return to the practices of the prewar.

La difícil topografía de la región favorecía la iniciativa local y, gracias a la ruptura con Stalin, la versión de Tito de la dictadura del proletariado ya no se veía obligada a reproducir exactamente todos los errores cometidos por la propia Unión Soviética en su camino hacia la modernidad industrial.

The difficult topography of the region favored the local initiative and, thanks to the break with Stalin, the Tito version of the dictatorship of the proletariat was no longer forced to reproduce exactly all the mistakes made by the Soviet Union itself in its path to industrial modernity.

Fueron estas consideraciones, más que el proyecto socialista creativo y alternativo que los admiradores occidentales de Tito ilusoriamente le atribuían, las que conformaron el modelo yugoslavo.

It was these considerations, more than the creative and alternative socialist project that the Western admirers of Tito illusory attributed to him, which formed the Yugoslav model.

Pero Yugoslavia seguía siendo diferente a pesar de todo: no necesariamente más amable con sus críticos, como Djilas y otros pudieron comprobar en sus propias carnes cuando discreparon de la ortodoxia titista[5], pero sí más flexible a la hora de gestionar las necesidades y los deseos de la población en general (en gran medida gracias a la ayuda occidental).

But Yugoslavia was still different in spite of everything: not necessarily friendlier with their critics, such as Djilas and others could verify in their own meats when they disagreed with titista orthodoxy [5], but more flexible when managing the needs and wishes of the population in general (largely thanks to Western aid).

Cuando la ensayista yugoslava Dubravka Ugrešić expresa su nostalgia por la Yugoslavia perdida de su juventud, lo que le viene a la mente son los «auténticos winklepickers (zapatos puntiagudos), los impermeables de plástico, la primera ropa interior de nylon… el primer viaje a Trieste».

When the Yugoslava essayist Dubravka Ugrešić expresses her nostalgia for the lost yugoslavia of her youth, which comes to mind are the "authentic Winklepickers (pointed shoes), plastic waterproof, the first nylon inside underwear ... the first trip to trieste."

Esta lista de artículos de consumo baratos hubiera ocupado un lugar mucho menos preponderante en la memoria búlgara o rumana, por ejemplo —y el «primer viaje a Trieste» habría estado fuera de toda cuestión—.

This list of cheap consumer items would have occupied a much less preponderant place in Bulgarian or Romanian memory, for example - and the "first trip to Trieste" would have been out of any matter.

The Yugoslavs were not prosperous or free; But they did not live locked in a hermetic system.

Nikita Jruschov, que sentía una profunda antipatía por Rumanía (un sentimiento muy extendido entre los rusos de su generación), había tratado de asignarle una función exclusivamente agrícola dentro de la distribución de tareas comunista a escala internacional.

Nikita Jruschov, who felt a deep antipathy by Romania (a very widespread feeling among the Russians of her generation), had tried to assign an exclusively agricultural function within the distribution of communist tasks to international scale.

Pero los dirigentes del Partido de Bucarest no tenían intención de verse reducidos a suministrar materias primas y alimentos a otras economías comunistas más prósperas y avanzadas.

But the leaders of the Bucharest party had no intention of being reduced to supplying raw materials and food to other more prosperous and advanced communist economies.

El hecho de haber desempeñado un papel complaciente en el encarcelamiento y la represión de los participantes en la revolución húngara aseguró a los rumanos la retirada de las fuerzas soviéticas de su territorio en 1958 y la posibilidad de emprender un camino cada vez más independiente.

The fact of having played a complacent role in the imprisonment and repression of the participants in the Hungarian revolution assured the Romanians withdrawal from the Soviet forces of their territory in 1958 and the possibility of undertaking an increasingly independent path.

Bajo el gobierno de Gheorghiu-Dej y (a partir de 1965) de Ceauşescu, Rumanía declinó implicarse en las peleas de Moscú con China e incluso se negó a autorizar al Pacto de Varsovia que realizara maniobras en su territorio.

Under the government of Gheorghiu-dej and (from 1965) of Ceauşescu, Romania declined to involve in Moscow's fights with China and even refused to authorize the Warsaw Pact to perform maneuvers in its territory.

Los líderes rumanos intentaron acercamientos con Tito (cuyas relaciones con el Pacto de Varsovia eran formales más que amistosas), en el marco de los cuales Gheorghiu-Dej incluso llegó a dirigirse a la Asamblea Nacional yugoslava en 1963; y financiaron la industrialización neoestalinista de Rumanía con dinero y maquinaria obtenidos de Europa occidental.

Romanian leaders tried approaches with Tito (whose relations with the Warsaw Pact were formal more than friendly), within the framework of which Gheorghiu-Dej even came to go to the Yugoslava National Assembly in 1963; and financed Romania's neoestalinist industrialization with money and machinery obtained from Western Europe.

Los acuerdos de Rumanía con Occidente se incrementaban constantemente, mientras que el comercio con los países del Comecon descendió, y pasó de representar el 70 por ciento del total del comercio exterior de Rumanía a comienzos de la década de 1960 a un 45 por ciento diez años después.

Romania agreements with the West were constantly increased, while commerce with Comecon countries decreased, and went from representing 70 percent of Romania's total foreign trade in the early 1960s to 45 percent ten years later.

Esta cacareada estrategia de «Rumanía primero» no resultaba impopular en el país —de hecho, una de las formas en las que el Partido Comunista de Rumanía en el poder había compensado sus orígenes claramente no rumanos había consistido en envolverse bajo el manto del nacionalismo.

This camed strategy of "Romania first" was not unpopular in the country - of fact, one of the ways in which the Romania Communist Party in power had compensated its origins clearly not Romanian had consisted of wrapping under the mantle of nationalism.

Gheorghiu-Dej fue el primero en hacerlo, y Ceauşescu se limitó a continuar con ella.

Gheorghiu-Dej was the first to do it, and Ceauşescu just continued her.

While Albania, the representative of China in Europe, did not exercise attraction to anyone except a few nostalgic stalinists and inveterate Maoists, the image of the communist Romania was curiously positive.

The Economist, in August 1966, called Ceauşescu "that of Gaulle de Europa del Este."

Precisely from Gaulle, on a visit to Bucharest held in May 1968, he had commented that although the communism of Ceauşescu was not appropriate for the West, Romania probably adapted well, "Chez vous vous a tel regime is useful, Car Il Fait marcher les et fait vamorz les choses" (For you, such a regime is useful, it makes people move and do things).

Without a doubt, De Gaulle was right in that Romanian communism would not have been appropriate for the West.

Romanian communism was especially ruthless and repressor: by distancing itself from the Soviet Union from 1958, Gheorghiu-dej and Ceauşescu also freed themselves from any need to emulate the de-stating and the reforms associated with the era of Jruscov.

A diferencia de otros Estados satélite, Rumanía no permitía ninguna oposición interna —los intelectuales de Bucarest de los años sesenta, aislados de su propia sociedad, no participaban en debates domésticos (eran inexistentes) y tenían que contentarse con leer los últimos nouveaux romans de París y participar indirectamente en una cultura francesa cosmopolita con la que los rumanos cultos siempre habían declarado mantener una especial afinidad.

Unlike other satellite states, Romania did not allow any internal opposition - Bucharest's intellectuals of the sixties, isolated from their own society, did not participate in domestic (were non -existent) debates and had to be content to read the latest Nouveaux Romans in Paris and participate indirectly in a French cosmopolitan culture with which the Romanian Romanians had always declared a special affinity.

Once Romania breached the Soviet veto and formally recognized West Germany in January 1967, relations became even more cordial: with his visit to Bucharest in August 1969, Richard Nixon would become the first president of the United States to visit a communist state.

El comunismo nacionalista —«tal vez sea un comunista, pero es nuestro comunista»— le resultó rentable a Ceauşescu: llegado el momento, Rumanía fue el primer Estado del Pacto de Varsovia en entrar en el GATT (en 1971), el Banco Mundial y el FMI (1972), en entablar relaciones comerciales preferentes con la Comunidad Económica Europea (1973) y en entrar en la lista de naciones más Favorecidas de Estados Unidos (1975)[6].

The nationalist communism - "perhaps she is a communist, but she is our communist" - it was profitable to Ceauşescu: when the time came, Romania was the first state of the Warsaw Covenant to enter the GATT (in 1971), the World Bank and the IMF (1972), in establishing commercial relations preferent with the European Economic Community (1973) and in entering the list of more favored nations of the United States of the United States (1975) [6].

Restless to the perspective of a repetition of the 1956 disturbances, the party's dome treated any criticism of its policies as a direct threat to its monopoly of power.

The result was a deep frustration among the intellectuals "revisionists", not only before the regime in general, but also at the lost opportunity to find a new direction, the pending issue of the Polish October.

En el verano de 1964 dos estudiantes de postgrado de la Universidad de Varsovia, Jacek Kuroń y Karol Modzelewski, elaboraron una crítica académica del sistema político y económico de la República Popular de Polonia.

In the summer of 1964 two postgraduate students from the University of Warsaw, Jacek Kuroń and Karol Modzelewski, elaborated an academic criticism of the political and economic system of the People's Republic of Poland.

Su disertación era impecablemente marxista en su tono y su contenido, pero eso no evitó que fueran expulsados del Partido y de la Unión de la Juventud Socialista, y que fueran denunciados en los círculos oficiales por difundir propaganda contraria al Partido.

His dissertation was impeccably Marxist in his tone and content, but that did not prevent them from being expelled from the party and the union of socialist youth, and that they were denounced in official circles for spreading propaganda contrary to the party.

Su respuesta consistió en publicar una Carta Abierta al Partido, que presentaron en las oficinas del Partido en la Universidad de Varsovia en marzo de 1965.

His response was to publish an open letter to the party, which they presented at the party offices at the University of Warsaw in March 1965.

En dicha Carta, los autores describían un régimen burocrático, autocrático, insensible a los intereses de nada que no fuera la élite dirigente a la que servía, que gobernaba de forma incompetente sobre una población trabajadora empobrecida y censuraba cualquier comentario o crítica.

In this letter, the authors described a bureaucratic, autocratic regime, insensitive to the interests of anything other than the leading elite to which it served, which ruled incompetently on an impoverished working population and censored any comments or criticism.

La única esperanza de Polonia, concluían Kuroń y Modzelewski, residía en una revolución auténtica, basada en los consejos de trabajadores, la libertad de prensa y la abolición de la policía política.

The only hope of Poland, concluded Kuroń and Modzelewski, resided in an authentic revolution, based on the Councils of Workers, Freedom of the Press and the abolition of the Political Police.

Al día siguiente de presentar su Carta, ambos fueron arrestados y acusados de promover el derrocamiento del Estado.

The day after presenting their letter, both were arrested and accused of promoting the overthrow of the State.

El 19 de julio de 1965 fueron sentenciados a penas de prisión de tres y tres años y medio respectivamente.

On July 19, 1965 they were sentenced to imprisonment for three and three and a half years respectively.

Las autoridades reaccionaron con especial preocupación a los términos impecablemente marxistas de su crítica, su eficaz utilización de los datos sociales para poner de relieve los desastrosos resultados económicos del régimen y su llamamiento a una revolución de los trabajadores para sustituir la dictadura burocrática de aquel momento (un matiz neotrotskista que no favorecería en nada su causa[7]).

The authorities reacted with special concern to the impeccably Marxist terms of their criticism, their effective use of social data to highlight the disastrous economic results of the regime and its call to a revolution of workers to replace the bureaucratic dictatorship of that time (a neotrotskyist nuance that would not favor its cause [7]).
