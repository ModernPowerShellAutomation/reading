Cuando estos tratados se ratificaran y entraran en vigor, el estatuto de la ocupación alemana caducaría a todos los efectos y, a todos los efectos, salvo nominalmente, los aliados occidentales habrían firmado la paz formal con su antiguo enemigo.

When these treaties were ratified and entered into force, the Statute of the German occupation would expire to all purposes and, to all purposes, except nominally, the western allies would have signed the formal peace with their former enemy.

Allied troops would remain in the Federal Republic to protect themselves from a possible recidivism, but as part of a European presence and by mutual agreement.

La acogida que los franceses prestaron a estos nuevos planes no fue en absoluto unánime, pero al haber echado abajo sus propias propuestas alternativas, no estaban en situación de protestar, aun cuando Alemania Occidental obtenía unas condiciones más generosas en virtud de los Tratados de 1954 que las que habría conseguido mediante el Plan Pleven.

The reception that the French lent to these new plans was not at all unanimous, but having thrown down their own alternative proposals, they were not in a situation of protesting, even though Western Germany obtained more generous conditions by virtue of the 1954 treaties than those that it would have achieved through the Pleven Plan.

No era la primera vez que la propia Francia se convertía en su peor enemigo en una disputa internacional.

It was not the first time that France herself became her worst enemy in an international dispute.

Comprensiblemente, el apoyo francés a los Tratados de París fue claramente ambiguo.

Understandably, French support for Paris's treaties was clearly ambiguous.

When the National Assembly celebrated the vote to ratify them, on December 30, 1954, the result was 287-260, that is, they were approved by a difference of only 27 votes.

Si los franceses se mostraban dubitativos, los rusos se sentían directamente contrariados.

If the French were hesitant, the Russians felt directly contrary.

El 15 de mayo de 1955, diez días después de la incorporación formal de Alemania Occidental a la OTAN y de la abolición de la Alta Comisión Aliada para la República Federal, la Unión Soviética anunció la formación de su Pacto de Varsovia.

On May 15, 1955, ten days after the formal incorporation of Western Germany to NATO and the abolition of the high allied commission for the Federal Republic, the Soviet Union announced the formation of its Warsaw Pact.

Polonia, Checoslovaquia, Hungría, Rumanía, Bulgaria, Albania y la Unión Soviética constituyeron una alianza de «amistad, cooperación y asistencia mutua» bajo un mando unificado.

Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, Albania and the Soviet Union constituted an alliance of "friendship, cooperation and mutual assistance" under a unified command.

Moscú derogó sus tratados de alianza de la guerra con Gran Bretaña y Francia y, tras aceptar lo inevitable, declaró la plena soberanía de la República Democrática de Alemania del Este y la incorporó al Pacto de Varsovia.

Moscow repealed his war alliance treaties with Great Britain and France and, after accepting the inevitable, declared the full sovereignty of the Democratic Republic of East Germany and incorporated it into the Warsaw Pact.

La cuestión alemana no había quedado exactamente resuelta; pero, con ambas partes integradas plenamente en sus respectivas alianzas internacionales, quedaría por el momento apartada, hasta ser reemplazada a su debido tiempo por el dilema aún pendiente de la anterior capital ahora dividida, Berlín.

The German issue had not been exactly resolved; But, with both parties fully integrated into their respective international alliances, it would be at the time away, until it was replaced in due time by the dilemma still pending the previous capital now divided, Berlin.

Una vez el futuro inmediato de Alemania había sido resuelto, ambos bandos se apresuraron a abordar otros conflictos y tensiones de carácter secundario.

Once the immediate future of Germany had been resolved, both sides hastened to address other secondary conflicts and tensions.

Los nuevos hombres del Kremlin y, en particular, Nikita Jruschov, se tomaban muy en serio su agenda de una «coexistencia pacífica» en Europa y compartían el deseo norteamericano de minimizar los riesgos de futuras confrontaciones.

The new Kremlin men and, in particular, Nikita Jrushchov, took their schedule for a "peaceful coexistence" in Europe very seriously and shared the American desire to minimize the risks of future confrontations.

El día después de anunciarse el Pacto de Varsovia, las cuatro potencias ocupantes firmaron el Tratado del Estado de Austria.

The day after the Warsaw Pact was announced, the four occupant powers signed the Austrian State Treaty.

Austria sería independiente y neutral, libre de seguir su propio camino sin alinearse ni con la OTAN ni con el Pacto de Varsovia[5].

Austria would be independent and neutral, free to follow its own path without aligning with NATO or with the Warsaw Pact [5].

Los cuatro ejércitos de ocupación debían retirarse —aunque la Unión Soviética, que ya había obtenido unos 100 millones de dólares de su zona de ocupación de la Austria oriental, se aseguró su tajada final con la imposición a Austria de la obligación de «comprar» los intereses económicos soviéticos en el sector este del país por 150 millones de dólares más.

The four occupation armies had to withdraw - although the Soviet Union, which had already obtained about 100 million dollars from its occupation zone of Eastern Austria, secured its final slice with the imposition to Austria of the obligation to "buy" the Soviet economic interests in the east sector of the country for 150 million dollars more.

Mientras, al sur de Austria, Yugoslavia e Italia habían acordado poner fin a su conflicto sobre Trieste.

Meanwhile, to southern Austria, Yugoslavia and Italy had agreed to end their conflict over Trieste.

Según un acuerdo gestionado por los estadounidenses y los británicos en octubre de 1954, la ciudad de Trieste permanecería en Italia, mientras que el territorio circundante, poblado mayoritariamente por eslovenos, sería devuelto a Yugoslavia.

According to an agreement managed by the Americans and the British in October 1954, the city of Trieste would remain in Italy, while the surrounding territory, mostly populated by Slovenos, would be returned to Yugoslavia.

Los acuerdos de Trieste, como tantas cosas durante aquellos años, se llevaron a cabo en el entendimiento de que serían considerados «provisionales»: en palabras del embajador italiano ante Estados Unidos, Alberto Tarchiani, el acuerdo de Trieste «no era provisional más que en apariencia, ya que en realidad era definitivo».

Trieste's agreements, like so many things during those years, were carried out in the understanding that they would be considered "provisional": in the words of the Italian ambassador to the United States, Alberto Tarchiani, Trieste's agreement "was not provisional more than in appearance, since it was actually definitive."

Los acuerdos sobre Austria, Yugoslavia e Italia fueron posibles gracias a un nuevo talante de detente en los asuntos europeos, simbolizado por la Cumbre de Ginebra celebrada en julio de 1955 (la primera desde Potsdam) y la admisión de dieciséis nuevos Estados miembros en las Naciones Unidas, que puso fin a un punto muerto de diez años entre el Este y el Oeste.

Austrian agreements, Yugoslavia and Italy were possible thanks to a new holder in European affairs, symbolized by the Geneva Summit held in July 1955 (the first from Potsdam) and the admission of sixteen new Member States in the United Nations, which ended a dead point of ten years between the east and west.

Más allá del ambiente de amistosos encuentros entre Eisenhower, Jruschov y Eden, la cuestión más importante que quedó resuelta en Ginebra fue la del destino de los aproximadamente 10.

Beyond the environment of friendly encounters between Eisenhower, Jrushchov and Eden, the most important issue that was resolved in Geneva was that of the destination of the approximately 10.

The Danes obtained an agreement on some lower border conflicts and compensation for German war crimes in 1955 and the Belgians a year later (the great Duchy of Luxembourg, however, did not reach an agreement with the Germans until 1959, and the Dutch until 1960).

Sin que en realidad nadie lo explicitara, la herida de los crímenes y los castigos de la guerra europea y sus secuelas se estaba cerrando.

Without really no one explained it, the wound of the crimes and punishments of the European War and its sequelae was closing.

Estos significativos avances se desarrollaban con el telón de fondo de una carrera armamentística de dimensiones considerables.

These significant advances were developed with the backdrop of an arms race of considerable dimensions.

Esta paradoja de que una Europa pacífica fuera tomando forma mientras las dos grandes potencias del momento se armaban hasta los dientes y se preparaban para la eventualidad de una guerra nuclear no es tan extraña como podría parecer.

This paradox that a peaceful Europe was taking shape while the two great powers of the moment were armed to the teeth and prepared for the eventuality of a nuclear war is not as strange as it might seem.

El creciente énfasis de Estados Unidos y la Unión Soviética en la estrategia sobre armas nucleares y los misiles intercontinentales que permitirían arrojarlas liberaba a los Estados europeos de la necesidad de competir en un terreno en el que no podían soñar con igualar los recursos de las superpotencias, aunque la Europa central siguiera constituyendo el campo de batalla más probable.

The increasing emphasis of the United States and the Soviet Union in the Strategy on Nuclear Weapons and the Intercontinental Missiles that would allow them to throw them liberated the European states from the need to compete in a field in which they could not dream of matching the resources of the superpowers, although the central Europe continued to constitute the most probable battlefield.

For this reason, the Cold War in Western Europe was experienced during those years in a very different way from that of the United States or even the USSR.

The United States Nuclear Arsenal had grown rapidly during the 1950s.

The number of nuclear weapons available to the US Armed Forces had gone from 9 in 1946, 50 in 1948 and 170 at the beginning of the decade, to 841 in 1952, until reaching a total of about 2000 at the time of Germany entry into NATO (seven years later, on the eve of the Cuba missile crisis, it would count 28.

Dada la abrumadora ventaja que la Unión Soviética tenía en Europa en cuanto a efectivos humanos y armas convencionales, estas armas nucleares aerotransportadas se convirtieron inevitablemente en la clave de la estrategia de Washington, sobre todo a raíz de la orden secreta emitida por Truman el 10 de marzo de 1950 de acelerar la fabricación de una bomba de hidrógeno.

Given the overwhelming advantage that the Soviet Union had in Europe in terms of human personnel and conventional weapons, these airborne nuclear weapons inevitably became the key to Washington's strategy, especially following the secret order issued by Truman on March 10, 1950 to accelerate the manufacture of a hydrogen pump.

Truman's decision was motivated by the success of the atomic bomb test carried out by the Soviet Union in August 1949.

The difference between the American nuclear capacity and the Soviet was reduced: the first thermonuclear test carried out successfully by the United States took place in the Elagelab Atoll, in the Pacific Ocean, on November 1, 1952; The first of this type made by the Soviet Union, in Semipalátinsk, was announced only ten months later, on August 12, 1953.

The coincidence of NATO nuclearization with the stabilization of the continent was not accidental.

Also from the point of view of the Soviet Union, conventional war in central and western Europe was losing strategic interest.

Kennedy in 1960, during his campaign to the presidency of the United States, was a myth, a successful Soviet propaganda exercise; The same can be said of the widely disseminated versions of the educational and technical superiority of the Soviet Union.

Two decades before the German Chancellor Helmut Schmidt made the comment, Jrushchov and some of his highest rank colleagues already understood that the empire they governed was basically a "high volta with missiles."

As Eisenhower had reiterated in 1953, the presence of the United States in Europe never intended to be anything other than "a provisional measure to convey confidence and security to our overseas friends."
