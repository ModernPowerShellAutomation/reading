The new secretary was young (he was 47 years old, 16 less than Novotný), belonged to the reformist wing of the party and, above all, was Slovak.

The first steps of Dubček seemed to confirm this impression: one month after his appointment the dome of the party granted its unequivocal approval to the postponed economic reform program.

El estilo más bien ingenuo de Dubček conectaba especialmente bien con los jóvenes, mientras que su indiscutible lealtad al Partido y al «socialismo» resultaba de momento tranquilizadora para el Kremlin y otros líderes comunistas pendientes de cada uno de sus gestos.

Dubček's rather naive style connected especially well with young people, while his unquestionable loyalty to the party and "socialism" resulted in reassuring for the Kremlin and other pending communist leaders of each of their gestures.

At first this ambiguity played in his favor, given that the different factions competed to obtain their support and offered to strengthen their position.

En los mítines celebrados en Praga durante las semanas siguientes a su elección, se exigía el final de la censura, una mayor libertad de prensa y una verdadera investigación sobre las purgas de la década de 1950 y las responsabilidades de la vieja guardia cercana a Novotný (que siguió como presidente del país hasta incluso después de haber sido expulsado de la dirección del Partido).

In the rallies held in Prague during the following weeks, the end of censorship, greater freedom of the press and a real investigation into the purges of the 1950s and the responsibilities of the old guard near Novotný was required (which continued as president of the country until even after having been expelled from the direction of the party).

Llevado por esta ola de entusiasmo popular, Dubček respaldó el llamamiento a la relajación de la censura e inició una purga de los partidarios de Novotný del Partido y del ejército checo.

Taken by this wave of popular enthusiasm, Dubček supported the call to the relaxation of censorship and initiated a purge of the novotný supporters of the Czech army.

El 22 de marzo Novotný dimitió a regañadientes del cargo de presidente y fue sustituido una semana más tarde por el general Ludvík Svoboda.

On March 22 Novotný reluctantly resigned from the position of president and was replaced a week later by General Ludvík Svoboda.

Cinco días después el Comité Central adoptó un «Programa de Acción» que exigía la igualdad de estatus y la autonomía de Eslovaquia, la rehabilitación de las víctimas del pasado y la «democratización» del sistema político y económico.

Five days later the Central Committee adopted an "action program" that demanded the equality of status and the autonomy of Slovakia, the rehabilitation of the victims of the past and the "democratization" of the political and economic system.

De esta manera el Partido respaldaba oficialmente lo que el Programa denominaba «un experimento único en el comunismo democrático»: el «socialismo con rostro humano», como se le conocería popularmente.

In this way the party officially supported what the program called "a unique experiment in democratic communism": "socialism with a human face", as it would be popularly known.

Durante un cierto tiempo (el documento hablaba de una transición de diez años) el Partido Comunista Checoslovaco permitiría la emergencia de otros partidos con los que competiría en unas verdaderas elecciones.

For a certain time (the document talked about a ten -year transition) the Czechoslovaco Communist Party would allow the emergence of other parties with which it would compete in true elections.

Estas ideas no eran en realidad originales, pero el hecho de que fueran pronunciadas públicamente por los órganos oficiales de un Partido Comunista gobernante desencadenó un terremoto político.

These ideas were not really original, but the fact that they were publicly pronounced by the official bodies of a ruling communist party triggered a political earthquake.

La Primavera de Praga había comenzado.

Prague spring had begun.

Los hechos acaecidos en Checoslovaquia en la primavera y el verano de 1968 giraron en torno a tres ilusiones del momento: la primera, extendida por todo el país a raíz del ascenso de Dubček, y especialmente a partir de la publicación del Programa de Acción, era que las libertades y reformas que en aquel momento se estaban discutiendo podían integrarse dentro del modelo «socialista» (es decir, comunista).

The facts that occurred in Czechoslovakia in spring and the summer of 1968 revolved around three illusions of the moment: the first, extended throughout the country following the rise of Dubček, and especially from the publication of the action program, was that the freedoms and reforms that were being discussed at that time could be integrated into the "socialist" model (that is, communist).

Sería erróneo suponer, desde la retrospectiva, que lo que los estudiantes, escritores y reformistas del Partido de 1968 estaban «verdaderamente» buscando era reemplazar al comunismo por el capitalismo liberal, o que su entusiasmo por el «socialismo con rostro humano» no fuera más allá de un compromiso o hábito retórico.

It would be wrong to assume, from the retrospective, that what the students, writers and reformists of the 1968 party were "truly" seeking was to replace communism with liberal capitalism, or that their enthusiasm for "socialism with human face" was not beyond a rhetorical commitment or habit.

Todo lo contrario: la idea de que existía una «tercera vía», un socialismo democrático compatible con unas instituciones libres, respetuoso con las libertades individuales y las metas colectivas había arraigado en la imaginación de los estudiantes checos tanto como lo había hecho en la de los economistas húngaros.

On the contrary: the idea that there was a "third way", a democratic socialism compatible with free institutions, respectful of individual freedoms and collective goals had rooted in the imagination of Czech students as much as it had done in that of Hungarian economists.

La distinción que ahora se hacía entre el desacreditado estalinismo de la generación de Novotný y el renovado idealismo de la era de Dubček gozaba de gran aceptación (incluso, y de hecho especialmente, entre los miembros del Partido[11]).

The distinction that was now between the discredited stalinism of the generation of Novotný and the renewed idealism of the era of Dubček enjoyed great acceptance (even, and in fact, especially, among the members of the party [11]).

As Jiří Pelikán affirmed in his prologue to a third report on Czech political trials (commissioned by Dubček in 1968 but suppressed after his fall), "the Communist Party has acquired enormous popularity and prestige, people have declared themselves spontaneously in favor of socialism [12]".

El 18 de abril quedó instaurado un nuevo Gobierno encabezado por Oldřich Černík, el cual, animado por las muestras públicas de afecto y apoyo (puestas especialmente de manifiesto durante las tradicionales celebraciones del primero de mayo), relajó prácticamente todos los controles oficiales sobre las expresiones de opinión públicas.

On April 18, a new government headed by Oldřich černík was established, which, encouraged by the public samples of affection and support (especially showing during the traditional celebrations of May 1), relaxed practically all official controls on public opinion expressions.

It appealed to the restoration of political parties, the formation of citizens committees to defend and promote the cause of reformism, and other proposals aimed at taking the initiative regarding other changes, outside the control of the party.
