4 The Eurasian map The epochs of international agitation, despite the way in which our assumptions about the endurability of the political map, lead to a review of the way in which geography is addressed, which is explained by being the basis of strategy and geopolitics.

La estrategia, tal como la definió Napoleón, es el arte de utilizar el tiempo y el espacio de un modo militar y diplomático.

The strategy, as Napoleon defined it, is the art of using time and space in a military and diplomatic way.

La geopolítica constituye el estudio del entorno al que se enfrenta cada Estado cuando ha de determinar su propia estrategia: ese entorno es la presencia de otros Estados que también luchan por su supervivencia y la consecución de beneficios.

Geopolitics constitutes the study of the environment that each State faces when it has to determine its own strategy: that environment is the presence of other states that also fight for their survival and the achievement of benefits.

Mackinder, father of the current geopolitics, who Morgenthau so much beloved, is famous not for a book, but for a single article, "The Geographical Pivot of History", published in the April number 1904 of the London magazine The Geographical Journal.

Mackinder defiende que Asia Central, al tiempo que forma parte del corazón continental de Eurasia, es el pivote sobre el que gira el destino de los grandes imperios del mundo, ya que el propio mapa de arterias naturales que atraviesan cordilleras y continúan a lo largo de valles fluviales fomenta el surgimiento de imperios, oficiales u oficiosos, en lugar de Estados.

Mackinder defends that Central Asia, while part of the continental heart of Eurasia, is the pivot on which the fate of the great empires of the world revolves, since the map of natural arteries that crosses mountain ranges and continues along river valleys encourages the emergence of empires, officers or offices, instead of states.
