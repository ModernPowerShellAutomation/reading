As for Ratzel, this was also deeply influenced by the work of Charles Darwin, and hence he develops an organic sense, to some biological extent, of geography, according to which the borders evolve constantly depending on the size and composition of the surrounding human populations.

Nosotros consideramos las fronteras como algo estático, como la representación última de la permanencia, la legalidad y la estabilidad, pero Ratzel solo veía en ellas una expansión, contracción e impermanencia graduales de las relaciones entre naciones.

We consider borders as something static, such as the ultimate representation of permanence, legality and stability, but Ratzel only saw in them an expansion, contraction and impermanence of gradual relations between nations.

For him, the map breathed as if it were a living being, and hence the idea of the organic-biological state whose expansion was endorsed by natural law.

Kjellén, nacionalista sueco a ultranza, temía el expansionismo ruso en su búsqueda de las aguas relativamente templadas del mar Báltico, por lo que abogaba por que una Suecia y una Finlandia expansionistas hicieran frente a los planes de Rusia.

Kjellén, a Swedish nationalist at ultranza, feared Russian expansionism in his search for the relatively temperate waters of the Baltic Sea, so he advocated that a Sweden and an expansionist Finland faced Russia's plans.

Nostálgico de la grandeza pasada de Suecia bajo los reinados de soberanos como Gustavo Adolfo y Carlos XII, Kjellén encontró apoyos entre los miembros de la aristocracia y la clase media alta, aunque sus ideas apenas calaron entre el público general.

Nostalgic of the last greatness of Sweden under the reigns of sovereigns such as Gustavo Adolfo and Carlos XII, Kjellén found support between the members of the aristocracy and the high middle class, although his ideas barely picked up among the general public.

Incluso a finales del siglo XIX y principios del XX, hacía tiempo que Escandinavia había abandonado sus ansias de poder, de modo que Kjellén acabó depositando todas sus esperanzas en una Gran Alemania que hiciera frente a Rusia y Gran Bretaña, naciones que detestaba especialmente.

Even at the end of the 19th and early twentieth centuries, Scandinavia had abandoned his desire for power, so that Kjellén ended up depositing all his hopes in a great Germany that he faced Russia and Great Britain, nations that he mostly hated.
