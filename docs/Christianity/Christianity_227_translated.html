Una de las curiosidades de la sociedad occidental desde la época de la Ilustración es que gran parte de su mejor música sacra (en modo alguno toda) ha sido obra de aquellos que abandonaron algún tipo de credo cristiano estructurado.

One of the curiosities of Western society since the time of the Enlightenment is that much of its best sacred music (in no way all) has been the work of those who abandoned some kind of structured Christian creed.

Edward Elgar, que creó el oratorio sagrado moderno más fabuloso del catolicismo inglés a partir del poema del cardenal Newman El sueño de Geroncio, exclamó en el momento de su primera representación que siempre había creído que «Dios era contrario al arte», y al final de su vida perdió la fe cristiana que le quedara.

Edward Elgar, who created the most fabulous Modern Oratory of English Catholicism from Cardinal Newman's poem The Dream of Geroncio, exclaimed at the time of his first representation that he had always believed that "God was contrary to art," and at the end of his life he lost the Christian faith that he had left.

Michael Tippett, que exploró la angustia del sufrimiento de la humanidad mediante los espirituales negros en A Child of Our Time, y que permaneció fiel a Agustín de Hipona y Mónica en el jardín de Ostia cuando salieron a vislumbrar a Dios en The Vision of St Augustine, jamás suscribió ninguna afirmación cristiana.

Michael Tippett, who explored the anguish of humanity's suffering through black spirituals in A Child of Our Time, and who remained faithful to Agustín de Hipona and Monica in the garden of Ostia when they went out to glimpse God in The Vision of St Augustine, never signed any Christian statement.

The agnosticism of Ralph Vaughan Williams, son of a cleric, did not prevent him from publishing the most exquisite hymn book in English, The English Hymnal, or creating an even greater splendor around the Christian tradition of the sacred verse in numerous choral and sung works; The passionate verse of the English poet and pastor George Herbert is almost inconceivable today without Vaughan Williams.

Even Nikolai Rimski-Kórsakov, which so much influenced the reconstruction of ecclesiastical music of Russian orthodoxy and remains one of the main ambassadors of orthodoxy outside its borders, was a virulent atheist.
