With the insistence of Cominform, in its newspapers and publications, about the "worsening of class struggle in the transition from capitalism to socialism", and in the "crucial role" of the party, there was a risk of reminding people that those had been precisely the policies of the Yugoslav party since 1945.

De ahí que dicha insistencia tuviera que ir siempre acompañada del énfasis en la lealtad a la Unión Soviética y a Stalin, el rechazo de todo camino «nacional» o «particular» hacia el socialismo y la necesidad de «redoblar la vigilancia».

Hence, this insistence had to always be accompanied by the emphasis on loyalty to the Soviet Union and Stalin, the rejection of every "national" or "particular" path towards socialism and the need to "redouble surveillance."

The old Reichsmark retired as a legal tender, each resident in Germany was allowed to change only forty of them for the new frames, a type of 1: 1, and from now on 10: 1.

A pesar de su impopularidad inicial (dado que destruía los ahorros, elevaba los precios reales y ponía los productos fuera del alcance de la mayoría de la gente), la moneda fue rápidamente aceptada cuando las tiendas se llenaron de artículos que los agricultores y los comerciantes ahora estaban dispuestos a vender a precios fijos por un instrumento de cambio fiable.

Despite its initial unpopularity (since it destroyed the savings, it raised real prices and put the products out of reach of most people), the currency was quickly accepted when stores were filled with items that farmers and merchants were now willing to sell at fixed prices for a reliable change instrument.

On June 23, the Soviet authorities responded with the issuance of a new German framework and the cut of the railroad lines that linked Berlin with Western Germany (three weeks later they would also close the channels).

Al día siguiente, el gobierno militar occidental de Berlín bloqueó los esfuerzos soviéticos por extender la nueva zona oriental hacia Berlín Oeste, una cuestión de principios, dado que la ciudad de Berlín estaba bajo el poder de cuatro países, y la zona occidental no había sido considerada por tanto como parte de la Alemania del Este ocupada por los soviéticos.

The next day, the Western Military Government of Berlin blocked the Soviet efforts to extend the new Eastern Zone towards Western Berlin, a matter of principles, given that the city of Berlin was under the power of four countries, and the western zone had not been considered therefore as part of the East Germany occupied by the Soviet.

Cuando las tropas soviéticas estrecharon su control sobre el transporte de superficie de la ciudad, los gobiernos estadounidense y británico decidieron abastecer sus zonas por vía aérea, y el 26 de junio aterrizó en el aeropuerto de Tempelhof de Berlín (Oeste) el primer avión de transporte.

When the Soviet troops strengthened their control over the city's surface transport, the American and British governments decided to supply their areas by air, and on June 26 it landed at the Berlin Tempelhof airport (west) the first transport plane.

El propósito de Stalin con el bloqueo de Berlín era obligar a Occidente a elegir entre abandonar la ciudad (aprovechándose de que en los protocolos de Potsdam no figuraba ninguna cláusula que garantizara por escrito el acceso de los aliados por superficie), o bien renunciar a sus planes de crear un Estado alemán occidental independiente.

Stalin's purpose with Berlin's blockade was to force the West to choose between abandoning the city (taking advantage of the fact that in the Potsdam protocols no clause was listed that guaranteed in writing the access of the allies by surface), or renouncing their plans to create an independent western German state.

Esto era lo que en realidad quería Stalin, dado que Berlín nunca había supuesto para él más que una baza para la negociación, aunque al final no consiguió ninguno de los dos objetivos.

This was what Stalin really wanted, since Berlin had never meant for him more than a asset for negotiation, although in the end he did not achieve either of the two goals.

Western allies not only managed to retain their part of Berlin (in a way to their own surprise and that of the grateful Berliners), but the Soviet blockade, and the immediately subsequent blow of Prague, only served to convince them even more of the need to move forward with their plans for Western Germany, and so that the division of the country was more acceptable for the Germans themselves.

France joined La Bizona in April 1949 to create a single economic entity of 49 million inhabitants (compared to only 17 million in the Soviet area).

As most Stalin's diplomatic initiatives, the blockade constituted an improvisation, not part of a calculated aggression plan (although at that time the West could hardly be blamed for not knowing it).

Los aliados occidentales no tenían intención de hacer semejante concesión, pero acordaron convocar una reunión para discutir el asunto, y el 12 de mayo la Unión Soviética puso fin al bloqueo a cambio tan solo de una conferencia de ministros de Asuntos Exteriores programada para el 23 de mayo.

The Western Allies had no intention of making such a concession, but agreed to convene a meeting to discuss the matter, and on May 12 the Soviet Union ended the blockade in return only from a conference of foreign ministers scheduled for May 23.

The conference was held in accordance with the provisions and lasted a month, but, as expected, no agreement was reached.

On September 15, 1949, after the success of his democraticistian party in the elections of the previous month, Konrad Adenauer became the first chancellor of the Republic.

Berlin's crisis produced three significant results.

The French were less involved in the Berlin crisis, because between July 18 and September 10, 1948, the country went through a political crisis, without a ruling majority in the National Assembly.
